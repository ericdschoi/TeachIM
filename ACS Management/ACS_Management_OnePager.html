2 · STEMI — time is myocardium

  • PCI-capable: primary PCI <90 min (onset <12h).
  • Non-PCI hospital: transfer if door-to-balloon <120 min; else fibrinolytics <90 min → transfer for angio 3–24h.
  • PCI > lytics (lytics fail ~35%, more death/MI/CVA/bleed).
  • Onset >12h: still PCI unless occluded infarct + no ischemia.

2025 ACC/AHA ACS update

  • P2Y12: ticagrelor OR prasugrel preferred over clopidogrel (PCI); DAPT ≥12 mo if not high bleed risk; ticagrelor monotherapy ≥1 mo an option.
  • Lipids: high-intensity statin ± ezetimibe for all; add non-statin (ezetimibe/PCSK9i/inclisiran/bempedoic acid) if LDL-C ≥70 on max statin.
  • Procedure: radial > femoral; intravascular imaging for complex lesions; complete revascularization (culprit-only in shock).
  • Transfusion to Hgb 10 reasonable if anemic & not bleeding; lipid panel 4–8 wk; cardiac rehab.

Do / Avoid & red flags

DO: ASA + anticoag + statin early; risk-stratify NSTEMI; radial access; complete revasc; rehab referral.
AVOID: routine O2 if SpO2≥90; morphine before max nitrates/BB; nitrates/BB in hypotension or RV infarct; delaying STEMI reperfusion; routine non-IRA PCI in cardiogenic shock.
RED FLAGS → immediate cath (<2h): cardiogenic shock, hemodynamic/electrical instability, refractory angina. Ongoing pain on max therapy = ongoing ischemia → revascularize.
