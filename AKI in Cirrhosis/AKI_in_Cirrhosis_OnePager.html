AKI IN CIRRHOSIS — Clinical One-Pager

Differentiate HRS from other AKI · ICA 2015 / AASLD 2021 · terlipressin FDA 2022 · IM resident reference

1 · DIFFERENTIAL — MOSTLY PRE-RENAL

Category%Causes
Pre-renal60–70Vol-responsive: GIB, over-diuresis, GI losses. Not vol-responsive: HRS, cardiorenal. Also sepsis (SBP, PNA, UTI), meds
Intrarenal30–40ATN, AIN, GN
Post-renal<1Obstruction, neurogenic bladder
Always, early: diagnostic paracentesis (SBP can be painless) + rule out GIB. AKI raises mortality 2–6× in cirrhosis.

2 · PATHOPHYS — A VASODILATORY STATE

↓Liver metabolism of vasodilators → splanchnic vasodilation → splanchnic pooling → ↓effective arterial volume (EAV).
CompensatedHeart ↑CO to defend perfusion
DecompensatedCO can't compensate → ↓BP → RAAS / ADH / sympathetic → renal vasoconstriction + Na/H₂O retention → hyponatremia + pre-renal AKI
HRS = the kidney squeezed by systemic vasodilation, not intrinsic renal disease.

3 · HRS-AKI — DIAGNOSIS (ICA)

  • Cirrhosis with ascites + AKI (ICA-AKI: ↑Cr ≥0.3 mg/dL in 48h or ≥50% in 7d)
  • NO improvement after diuretic withdrawal + albumin 1 g/kg/d (max 100 g) ×48h
  • No shock; no nephrotoxins; no structural dz (bland UA, no proteinuria/hematuria, normal renal US)
Terminology update: old Type 1 → HRS-AKI (fixed Cr>2.5 threshold dropped); Type 2 → HRS-NAKI (often refractory ascites). ICA 2015 / AASLD 2021.

3b · HRS-AKI — TREATMENT

AlbuminExpand EAV (up to ~50 g/day)
VasoconstrictorTerlipressin (FDA 2022) or norepinephrine (ICU); midodrine 5–7.5 mg TID + octreotide 100 µg SQ TID + albumin = older ward regimen. Goal ↑MAP >15
TIPSDelayed renal benefit; most too ill to qualify
HDBridge to transplant
TransplantOnly definitive Rx (±combined liver-kidney)
Terlipressin — avoid: ACLF grade 3, pre-existing hypoxia, or SCr >5 mg/dL (respiratory-failure signal; benefit unlikely).

4 · SBP — DON'T MISS IT

Ascitic ≥250 PMN/µL = SBP → cefotaxime/ceftriaxone ×5d (FQ if PCN-allergic).
Albumin in SBP: 1.5 g/kg day 1 + 1 g/kg day 3 — reduces HRS & mortality; greatest benefit if Tbili >4, Cr >1, or BUN >30 (Sort, NEJM 1999).

URINE INDICES & PITFALLS

  • On diuretics → use FeUrea (<35% = pre-renal); FeNa is falsely high
  • Low urine Na / FeNa can occur even in ATN (RAAS reclaims Na); granular casts also seen w/ severe hyperbilirubinemia — both unreliable for ATN in cirrhosis
  • AIN clue: WBC casts + NSAID use → stop the drug
