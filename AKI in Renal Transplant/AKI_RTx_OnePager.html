AKI in a Kidney-Transplant Recipient

Differential = transplant-specific causes + the usual pre/intra/post-renal causes · let timing-from-transplant narrow it · toolbox: renal duplex US, BK/CMV PCR, CNI trough, biopsy + DSAs

5 transplant-specific causes (don't drop the usual ones)

CompartmentTransplant-specific
Pre-renalAcute CNI toxicity (afferent + efferent vasoconstriction); renal artery stenosis / artery-or-vein thrombosis
Intra-renalAcute rejection (cellular / antibody); viral (BK, CMV, adenovirus); chronic CNI toxicity; recurrent or de novo GN
Post-renalUreteral stenosis; extrinsic compression — lymphocele, perinephric hematoma, urinoma
Usual causes still apply: volume depletion, NSAIDs/ACEi, sepsis, ATN, AIN, contrast, obstruction (calculi/clot/cancer). Transplant causes are added, not substituted.

Timing from transplant narrows the differential

WhenThink first
< 24 hHyperacute rejection (pre-formed DSAs); vascular thrombosis
First 3 moAcute CNI toxicity; acute rejection (cellular/humoral)
3 mo – 2 yrRenal artery stenosis; BK nephropathy (peak ~10–13 mo); CMV nephritis (>3 mo)
Any timeLate rejection (esp. non-adherence); ureteral stenosis; usual AKI causes

The toolbox

  • Renal duplex US — hydronephrosis (post-renal) + arterial flow (RAS/thrombosis)
  • Viral studies — plasma + urine BK PCR, CMV PCR
  • CNI trough — supratherapeutic supports acute toxicity
  • Biopsy + DSAs (± donor-derived cell-free DNA) — the standard for rejection & chronic CNI tox
  • Involve transplant nephrology early

Do / AVOID

  • DO work the full pre/intra/post differential before reaching for transplant-specific tests
  • DO review meds — azole antifungals (e.g. fluconazole) raise CNI levels → acute toxicity
  • DO send plasma BK PCR (preferred) alongside urine; reduce immunosuppression for sustained DNAemia
  • AVOID trusting a normal CNI trough to rule out chronic CNI toxicity
  • AVOID attributing all dysfunction to rejection — biopsy + DSAs confirm before escalating immunosuppression
2024 update: plasma BK PCR screening (monthly to ~9 mo, then q3mo to 2 yr; 2nd Int'l Consensus, Transplantation 2024). dd-cfDNA helps exclude antibody-mediated rejection but does not replace biopsy (ASTS, Oct 2024).
