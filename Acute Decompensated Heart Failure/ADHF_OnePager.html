Acute Decompensated Heart Failure — Inpatient Management

Profile the patient first (wet/dry × warm/cold) → decongest with IV loops, dose by home dose → start/continue GDMT before discharge · the admission is the intervention

1 · Profile on arrival — congestion & perfusion

Two bedside axes drive every decision. Congestion (wet vs dry) and perfusion (warm vs cold). Most floor patients are warm-and-wet.

DryWet (congested)
Warm (perfused)Compensated — optimize GDMTWarm-wet: IV loop diuretic — the bread & butter admit
Cold (hypoperfused)Cold-dry: cautious fluid / inotropeCold-wet: diuresis + consider inotrope/vasodilator; think ICU
Congestion signs: JVD, orthopnea, edema, weight gain, B-lines/POCUS. Hypoperfusion: narrow pulse pressure, cool extremities, ↑lactate, ↑Cr, somnolence. Hypoperfusion ≠ hypotension.

2 · Decongest — IV loop diuretics

  • Give IV, not PO — gut edema cripples PO absorption.
  • Dose: IV furosemide at ≥ 1–2.5× the total home daily PO dose, given IV BID (DOSE trial). Naive patient: 40–80 mg IV.
  • Check response at 2 h (spot urine Na > 50–70 mmol/L or UOP > 100–150 mL/h = good). If inadequate, double the dose — don't just wait.
  • Inadequate on high-dose loop → add thiazide (metolazone / IV chlorothiazide) for sequential nephron blockade; consider acetazolamide (ADVOR) or early SGLT2i.
  • Target net negative ~1–2 L/day; daily weights, strict I/O, BMP q12–24h.
A rising creatinine during effective decongestion is often hemoconcentration, not injury — don't reflexively stop diuresis if the patient is still wet and making urine.

3 · GDMT — start it during admission

The four pillars of HFrEF (EF ≤ 40%). Initiate/continue before discharge — admission is the highest-yield moment (STRONG-HF).

PillarAgentInpatient note
ARNI/ACEi/ARBsacubitril-valsartanHold for SBP <90, K >5.5, AKI; 36 h washout from ACEi
Beta-blockercarvedilol, metoprolol succ.Continue if already on; don't start until euvolemic & off inotropes

SGLT2idapagliflozin, empagliflozinStart in-hospital — safe, speeds decongestion (EMPULSE)
Don't stop a chronic beta-blocker just for an admission unless the patient is cold/hypoperfused — withdrawal worsens outcomes. Up-titrate GDMT at close follow-up.

Precipitants — find the FAILURE

  • Forgetting meds / Fluid & salt · Arrhythmia (AF/RVR) · Ischemia/Infection · Lifestyle (Na, EtOH) · Upregulation (thyroid, pregnancy, anemia) · Renal/Rx (NSAIDs, gabapentinoids, dihydropyridines) · Embolus (PE)
  • Work-up: ECG + troponin, BNP/NT-proBNP, CBC, BMP, TSH, CXR; echo if no recent EF.

Discharge bundle & Do/Avoid

  • DO transition IV→PO diuretic & observe 24 h euvolemic before discharge
  • DO document EF, dry weight, discharge diuretic dose, and the 4 GDMT pillars
  • DO arrange 7-day follow-up + daily-weight teaching
  • AVOID discharging still-congested ('looks better') — residual congestion drives readmission
  • AVOID NSAIDs, glitazones, most non-DHP CCBs in HFrEF
  • AVOID starting/uptitrating beta-blocker while still wet or hypoperfused
