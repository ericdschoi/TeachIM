Step 1b — History on everyone

  • CV risk: HTN, DM, AF, CAD, smoking (↑ posterior-circ stroke)
  • Hearing loss / tinnitus (Meniere, labyrinthitis)
  • Focal neuro sx, new headache/neck pain
  • Meds/changes: AEDs, sedatives, antiHTN, analgesics
  • Substance use; recent trauma/barotrauma

Step 2 — Dix-Hallpike (positional)

  • Head 45° to side, lay supine, head 20° off table; watch 30 s; repeat other side
  • Positive = transient upbeat-torsional nystagmus + vertigo = pc-BPPV
  • Sens ~79% / Spec ~75% — better at ruling IN
  • Treat now: Epley canalith-repositioning (strong rec, GRACE-3). Half-somersault = home option, less effective. Vestibular PT if refractory.
  • Negative → orthostatics (SBP ↓≥20 / DBP ↓≥10 within 3 min), reconsider

Step 3 — HINTS (continuous AVS only)

Use ONLY in continuous, spontaneous vertigo with nystagmus, by a trained examiner.

ComponentPeripheral (OK)Central (bad)
Head ImpulseCatch-up saccade (abnl VOR)Normal / intact (no saccade)
NystagmusHorizontal, unidirectionalBidirectional or vertical
SkewNoneVertical skew (cover test)

Rule: ANY central finding → stroke. ALL reassuring → vestibular neuritis. Mnemonic INFARCT = Impulse Normal, Fast-phase Alternating, Refixation on Cover Test.

Management by diagnosis

  • BPPV: Epley at bedside; half-somersault home; PT if refractory. No imaging.
  • Vestibular neuritis: antiemetics + short-course vestibular suppressant/anticholinergic (limit to days — chronic use blocks central compensation). Steroids only via shared decision-making if ≤3 days (weak evidence).
  • Central / stroke pattern: activate stroke pathway; MRI-DWI + MRA; ASA + statin; tPA/thrombectomy per window & resources.
  • Acute idiopathic SNHL: steroids ARE indicated.

Red flags → central work-up

  • Normal head impulse WITH continuous vertigo
  • Direction-changing or vertical/torsional nystagmus; skew
  • Truncal ataxia — can't sit/stand unaided
  • New headache / neck pain; any focal deficit
  • Vascular risk factors + sudden persistent vertigo
