Acute Liver Failure (ALF) — Clinical One-Pager

Facilitator quick reference · TeachIM (Mowry/Kriss, Apr 2022), optimized Jun 2026 · CC BY-NC 4.0 · clinician-review before use
ALF = ALL three: acute liver injury (<26 wk, no chronic liver dz*) + coagulopathy (INR >1.5) + hepatic encephalopathy. HE is what makes it "failure." *exc: HBV, AIH, Wilson's.

Definition & HE Grades

CriterionDetail
Injury↑AST/ALT, bili; <26 wk; no chronic dz
CoagulopathyINR >1.5 (key prognostic marker)
EncephalopathyWest Haven I–IV; defines ALF

HE grades: I sleep-wake/attention · II lethargy+asterixis · III stupor/gross disorient · IV coma. Gr III–IV → intubate.

Etiologies & Work-up

CategoryCauses / work-up
Drugs/toxins (~50%)APAP (lead), supplements, Amanita; statins/AEDs/abx. APAP+salicylate lvl, utox, LiverTox
ViralHep A/B/D/E; rare HSV/VZV. Serologies+PCR, HIV
Ischemic/vascularBudd-Chiari, shock liver. Liver US doppler
OtherAIH, preg (HELLP/AFLP), Wilson's. ANA/ASMA/IgG, preg test, ceruloplasmin+24h Cu, bx

Alcohol does NOT cause ALF (can worsen another insult). Wilson's clue: ALP:Tbili <4, young, Coombs-neg hemolysis.

ICU Management (4 steps)

1. Complications:

ProblemAction
Cerebral edemaq1-2h neuro checks; HOB>30; hypertonic saline/mannitol; MINIMIZE IVF; NH3 >150-200 = high ICH risk
Hypoglycemiaq1-2h glucose; dextrose infusion
MOFVent/pressors/RRT; TTE r/o cardiogenic
CoagulopathyVit K ok; FFP/factors ONLY if bleeding
InfectionHigh risk; consider empiric abx/antivirals

2. NAC empirically in ALL ALF (benefit even non-APAP, best early/low-grade HE). 3. Prognosticate KCC (±MELD) w/ hepatology. 4. TRANSFER early to LT center.

Pearls

  • No HE = acute liver INJURY, not failure.
  • Don't reflexively correct INR — it's your prognostic compass.
  • Go easy on IVF even with rising Cr — volume worsens cerebral edema.
  • KCC is specific, not sensitive — a negative KCC should NOT defer transfer.
  • Transfer EARLY, before progression precludes safe transport.
