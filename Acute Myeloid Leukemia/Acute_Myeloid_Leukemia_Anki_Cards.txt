How common is AML and who gets it?	~1% of new cancers (~20k/yr in the US); predominantly elderly (median age 68, ~59% are >65). Any age can be affected.
AML prognosis with and without treatment?	Without treatment: survival is days to weeks. With treatment: only ~1 in 3 (≈30%) reach 5-year survival.
Where is the pathology in AML?	Overproliferation of myeloid stem cells OR myeloblasts. Because these are precursors to RBCs, WBCs, and platelets, any CBC pattern is possible.
Three CBC patterns AML can produce?	Isolated leukocytosis (myeloblast → ↑WBC), leukocytosis + cytopenias (blasts crowd out/disrupt other lines), and pancytopenia (myeloid stem cell affected → proportionate declines).
Diagnostic criteria for AML?	(1) ≥20% blasts in bone marrow aspirate OR peripheral blood, AND (2) cells of myeloid origin (Auer rods, MPO+, or myeloid immunophenotype). Certain defining cytogenetic abnormalities [t(6;9), CBF] are diagnostic regardless of blast count.
How did WHO/ICC/ELN 2022 change AML diagnosis?	The ≥20% blast cutoff is no longer required when an AML-defining genetic abnormality is present (e.g., PML::RARA, core-binding factor). NPM1-mutated AML is a distinct entity needing ≥10% blasts. Greater emphasis on MRD by NGS/flow.
What finding on smear is diagnostic of AML?	Auer rods (needle-like azurophilic cytoplasmic inclusions).
Physical exam findings of AML?	Gingival hyperplasia and leukemia cutis (leukemic infiltration); Sweet syndrome (acute febrile neutrophilic dermatosis); conjunctival/palmar pallor (anemia); petechiae/purpura (thrombocytopenia).
The four critical complications of AML?	Leukostasis, tumor lysis syndrome (TLS), disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC), and febrile neutropenia.
Leukostasis — mechanism, threshold, and treatment?	Hyperleukocytosis + cytokines → ↑viscosity → impaired perfusion (neuro, respiratory symptoms). Typically WBC >100k (can occur ≥50k with symptoms). Treat by reducing WBCs: cytoreductive chemo/hydroxyurea ± leukapheresis. Leukapheresis is CONTRAINDICATED in APL (worsens coagulopathy).
Tumor lysis syndrome — labs and treatment?	Hyperkalemia, hyperuricemia, hyperphosphatemia, HYPOcalcemia; risks AKI (crystal nephropathy) and arrhythmias. Prevent/treat with IV fluids + allopurinol; rasburicase for severe/high uric acid.
DIC in AML — labs and management?	↑PT/PTT/INR and ↑D-dimer with LOW fibrinogen (key); schistocytes on smear. Supportive transfusions temporize; definitive treatment is treating the leukemia. Strongly associated with APL.
Febrile neutropenia — definition and empiric antibiotics?	Single oral T >38.3°C (or ≥38.0°C sustained ≥1 h) with ANC <500 (or expected <500 within 48 h). Empiric anti-pseudomonal monotherapy: cefepime or piperacillin-tazobactam. Add MRSA coverage (vancomycin) only for catheter/SSTI/PNA infection or instability.
Why avoid acetaminophen in hospitalized neutropenic patients?	It can mask fever, which may be the only early sign of impending sepsis.
What is APL and why is it special?	Acute promyelocytic leukemia — distinct treatment with ATRA + arsenic trioxide; excellent long-term prognosis, but the acute phase (DIC/bleeding) is the deadliest period. Avoid leukapheresis.
ELN risk stratification and prognostic spread?	Sorts patients by cytogenetics + NGS into favorable / intermediate / adverse. 5-year OS roughly 46% (favorable) vs 18% (intermediate) vs 4% (adverse).
AML treatment framework?	Induction (treat detectable cancer: 7+3 = 7 days cytarabine + 3 days anthracycline; Ven/HMA for unfit/elderly; ATRA/arsenic for APL) → consolidation (allogeneic SCT or further chemo) → maintenance; salvage/targeted therapy or hospice for relapsed/refractory disease.
Why is the adjuvant/neoadjuvant framework not used in AML?	Hematologic malignancies are treated with systemic therapy only — there's no surgery/radiation backbone, so induction/consolidation/maintenance replaces adjuvant/neoadjuvant language.
Newer targeted/maintenance agents in AML (2022–23)?	Quizartinib added to 7+3 for newly diagnosed FLT3-ITD+ (QuANTUM-First, FDA 2023); ivosidenib+azacitidine for newly dx unfit IDH1 (AGILE); olutasidenib for R/R IDH1; oral azacitidine (Onureg) maintenance (QUAZAR); CPX-351 for t-AML/AML-MR.
