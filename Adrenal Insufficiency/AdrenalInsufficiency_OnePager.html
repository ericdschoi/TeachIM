Primary AI loses aldosterone → hypovolemia, ↑K, ↓Na → crisis-prone. Central spares RAAS.

Causes

  • Primary: autoimmune adrenalitis (most common); infection (TB, fungi, syphilis); hemorrhage; mets/tumor; drugs.
  • Central: exogenous glucocorticoids (most common cause overall); mass/craniopharyngioma/mets; XRT; infiltrative (hemochromatosis, sarcoid); Sheehan; surgery.
  • Drugs: steroids, azoles, etomidate, mitotane, checkpoint inhibitors; rifampin/phenytoin/PBT (↑metabolism).

Diagnosis

  • 1st test: 8 AM serum cortisol. Very low rules in; clearly high rules out; indeterminate → stim test.
  • Board cutoffs (older assay): <3 ruled-in; <10 suspected; >18 ruled-out.
  • Cutoffs are ASSAY-SPECIFIC: modern monoclonal/LC-MS-MS read ~20-30% lower → rule-out / stim peak ~14-15 µg/dL, not 18.
  • ACTH (cosyntropin) stim: 250 µg IV/IM, cortisol at 0 + 30-60 min; subnormal peak = AI.
  • ACTH level localizes: ↑ = primary; low/nl = central. Then 21-OH-Ab (primary) or pituitary MRI (central).

Chronic replacement

TypeRegimen
PrimaryHydrocortisone 20-25 mg/24h split (AM-weighted) + fludrocortisone 0.1 mg qAM ± DHEA
CentralHydrocortisone 15-20 mg/24h split; no fludrocortisone (RAAS intact)

At stress doses (HC >~50 mg/d) mineralocorticoid effect is adequate — fludro can be held.

Adrenal crisis — treat NOW

  • Presents: hypotension/shock ± fever, abd pain, N/V, fatigue; ± ↑K / ↓Na / hypoglycemia.
  • Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV bolus → 200 mg/24h (or 50 mg q6h) + aggressive IV fluids. Treat empirically — do NOT await testing.
  • If a stim test is still pending, use dexamethasone (no cortisol-assay cross-reaction).

Patient safety / sick-day rules

  • Double/triple the oral dose for febrile illness or stress; IV hydrocortisone for major stress/surgery.
  • Every AI patient: emergency IM hydrocortisone kit + medical-alert ID.
  • Under-replacement during stress is what precipitates crisis.
