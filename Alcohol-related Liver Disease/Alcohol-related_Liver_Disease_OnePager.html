Alcohol-related Liver Disease (ALD) — Clinical One-Pager

Facilitator quick reference · TeachIM (Mowry/Haydek, Nov 2022), optimized Jun 2026 · CC BY-NC 4.0 · clinician-review before use
Spectrum: hepatic steatosis → steatohepatitis (ASH) → fibrosis → cirrhosis. Steatosis & inflammation regress with abstinence; fibrosis is largely irreversible.

Stages & Risk Factors

StageFeatures / reversibility
Steatosis~90% of heavy drinkers; benign marker; reversible
Steatohepatitis (ASH)Inflammatory; indolent or acute AH; infl. reversible, fibrosis not
CirrhosisEnd-stage fibrosis; irreversible

Risk: ≥2 drinks/d (F), ≥3/d (M); binging; genetics; obesity; female sex; smoking; concurrent liver dz.

Diagnostic Evaluation

Screen all adults >18 w/ unhealthy use (CAGE, AUDIT-C). Initial: RUQ US + CMP, CBC, INR.

EntityKey features
SteatosisIncidental; LFTs nl/mild ↑; indistinguishable from MASLD → history clinches it
AHClinical dx; AST:ALT >2, both <400; ↑Tbili
Fibrosis/cirrhosisFIB-4, APRI, FibroTest, ELF; elastography/MRI; nodular liver

AST/ALT >400–500 → not pure AH: r/o ischemic, APAP, viral.

Acute Alcohol-related Hepatitis (AH)

Dx (AASLD clinical): heavy use (F ≥3 / M ≥4 drinks/d × ≥60 d), jaundice ≤8 wk, AST:ALT >2 (both <400 IU/L), Tbili >3 mg/dL. Often ↑WBC/PMN, RUQ pain. R/o infection in EVERY pt.

Severity: MDF (Tbili+INR) ≥32 or MELD >20 = severe (30–50% 30-d mortality).

Steroids: prednisolone 40 mg/d if no contraindication; contraindic: active infxn, AKI Cr>2.5, uncontrolled GIB, MOF/shock, alt cause. ± IV NAC (short-term survival, 2024 ACG).

Lille (day 4–7)Action
<0.45 responderComplete 28-d course (~85% 6-mo survival)
≥0.45 non-respStop steroids → palliative / LT eval

Pearls & 2024 Updates

  • Abstinence beats every drug; at steatosis the liver can fully recover.
  • Steroids in AH buy weeks, not years, and only in Lille responders.
  • 2024 ACG ALD guideline reaffirms MDF≥32 / MELD>20 + Lille rule; pred+NAC adjunct; early LT for select severe AH.
  • 2023 nomenclature: NAFLD→MASLD, NASH→MASH; MetALD = metabolic dysfunction + moderate alcohol.
  • STOPAH (2015): pentoxifylline no benefit; prednisolone short-term only.
