The "Hospital Trio"

Across every site, healthcare/hospital exposure adds MRSA, Pseudomonas, and Enterococcus. Escalate empirically only with risk factors; de-escalate by culture at 48–72 h.

Hospital-acquired triggers: onset >48 h after admission; hospitalization ≥48 h or care-facility stay within 90 d; broad-spectrum abx >5 d in prior 90 d.

Do / Avoid

  • DO draw cultures before/with first dose; narrow promptly.
  • DO use a respiratory FQ as a single-agent CAP option when beta-lactam+macrolide isn't feasible.
  • AVOID reflexive anaerobic coverage in aspiration (only abscess/empyema).
  • AVOID treating asymptomatic bacteriuria (except pregnancy / pre-urologic procedure).
  • AVOID antibiotics-without-surgery in NSTI.
