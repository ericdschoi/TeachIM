The 4 questions to ask of any antibiotic?	(1) GPCs (Staph/Strep; +MRSA? +Enterococcus?); (2) GNRs (+Pseudomonas? +ESBL?); (3) atypicals; (4) anaerobes (+B. fragilis/gut?).
General rules for penicillins?	No MRSA, no atypicals; GN coverage increases across classes (Pen -> antistaph -> amino+BLI -> pip-tazo).
One difference between amp-sulbactam and pip-tazo?	Pip-tazo adds Pseudomonas. Both cover GPCs, enteric GNRs, and gut anaerobes.
First-line for MSSA?	Antistaphylococcal penicillins (nafcillin, oxacillin, dicloxacillin) or cefazolin (1st-gen cephalosporin).
E. faecalis vs E. faecium ampicillin/VRE mnemonic?	E. faecaLESS = LESS resistant (~90% amp-sensitive, rarely VRE). E. faecium = more resistant (~10% amp-sensitive, more often VRE).
General rules for cephalosporins?	No Enterococcus, no atypicals; GN coverage increases by generation (except the special 5th).
Which cephalosporins cover Pseudomonas? Any oral?	Ceftazidime (3rd) and cefepime (4th). No oral cephalosporin covers Pseudomonas.
Which cephalosporin covers MRSA?	Ceftaroline ("5th generation") -- the only beta-lactam with MRSA activity.
Carbapenem general rules?	Broadest (GP/GN/anaerobe), most reliable ESBL; no MRSA/atypical, poor Enterococcus.
Which carbapenem lacks Pseudomonas coverage, and its niche?	Ertapenem -- once-daily, first-line for ESBL E. coli. Meropenem/imipenem/doripenem add Pseudomonas.
Cipro vs levo vs moxifloxacin?	Cipro = urinary (GN/PsA, poor Strep, not for respiratory). Levo = urinary+respiratory (adds S. pneumo). Moxi = respiratory, best anaerobe, weak PsA/poor renal.
Fluoroquinolone class harms?	QT prolongation, aortic aneurysm/dissection, tendon rupture, encephalopathy, C. difficile.
Aztreonam spectrum and the one allergy caveat?	Aerobic GN + Pseudomonas only (no GP/anaerobe/atypical). Minimal beta-lactam cross-reactivity except ceftazidime (shared side chain).
Aminoglycoside coverage and toxicities?	Aerobic GNR + Pseudomonas; no anaerobe/atypical. Oto- and nephrotoxic. Gentamicin adds GP synergy for enterococcal endocarditis.
Oral CA-MRSA options and a key caveat for each?	Clindamycin (antitoxin; C. diff; B. fragilis resistant), TMP-SMX (variable Strep -- add beta-lactam for nonpurulent cellulitis), doxycycline (minimal Strep).
IV HA-MRSA agents?	Vancomycin (1st-line; also Enterococcus/CoNS), daptomycin (NOT for pneumonia; rhabdo), linezolid (PO/IV; 1st-line VRE; often preferred for MRSA pneumonia).
Why not daptomycin for MRSA pneumonia?	Inactivated by pulmonary surfactant.
Newer MDR Gram-negative agents (escalate with ID)?	Cefiderocol (CRE/CRAB/Stenotrophomonas/PsA); ceftazidime-avibactam, ceftolozane-tazobactam, meropenem-vaborbactam, imipenem-relebactam; aztreonam-avibactam for metallo-beta-lactamases.
