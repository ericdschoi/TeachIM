Clostridioides difficile Infection (CDI) — Clinical One-Pager

Facilitator quick reference · TeachIM (Zhang, Sept 2021), optimized Jun 2026 · CC BY-NC 4.0 · clinician-review before use
Test ONLY symptomatic patients (≥3 unformed stools/24 h or ileus). Colonization is common (~21% inpatients) — do NOT test asymptomatic patients and do NOT test for cure.

Diagnosis (2-step)

TestDetects / performance
GDH AgEnzyme from ALL strains; high sens (94–96%)
Toxin A/BToxigenic strains; high spec (94–98%)
NAATToxin gene; confirms toxigenic — use for discordant results

GDH+ / toxin+ → CDI · both − → r/o · discordant → NAAT. No repeat test <7 d.

Severity Classification

ClassDefinition
Non-severeAbsence of severe criteria
SevereWBC >15k OR Cr ≥1.5 mg/dL
FulminantSevere + hypotension/shock OR ileus/megacolon
RecurrentSx + positive test within 8 wk (~20%)

RF: age >65, healthcare exposure, antibiotics; community-acq: IBD, cardiac dz, CKD.

Treatment

ScenarioRegimen
Non-severe / severe (initial)Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID ×10 d (preferred) or PO vanc 125 mg q6h ×10 d
FulminantPO vanc 500 mg q6h (PR if ileus) + IV metronidazole 500 mg q8h; surgery consult
1st recurrenceFidaxomicin (pref) or tapered/pulsed PO vanc
≥2 recurrencesTapered/pulsed vanc, vanc→rifaximin, or FMT/microbiota — refer

Metronidazole PO only if low-risk non-severe & neither agent available.

Recurrence Prevention & Precautions

  • Microbiota therapy (post-course): Rebyota (rectal, 2022), Vowst (oral spores, 2023), or FMT.
  • Bezlotoxumab discontinued Jan 2025 — no longer available.
  • High-risk on systemic abx: consider PO vanc 125 mg daily ppx (continue 5 d after abx).
  • Contact + enteric precautions until 48 h after diarrhea resolves.
  • NOT recommended: probiotics; stopping PPIs for prevention.

Pearls

  • A positive test ≠ infection — symptoms make the diagnosis.
  • IV vancomycin does NOT treat CDI (no luminal delivery).
  • No test-of-cure: shedding persists ~56%; post-infectious IBS up to 25%.
  • Community-acquired CDI (~40%) is rising — consider it in younger patients.
