COPD Exacerbation — Inpatient Management

Bronchodilators + steroids + targeted antibiotics + the right oxygen device · NIV is the single biggest mortality lever · treat the cause, set up the discharge to prevent the next one

1 · Define & triage

An exacerbation is an acute worsening of dyspnea, cough, and/or sputum (volume or purulence) beyond day-to-day variation. GOLD 2026 grades severity by measured physiology, not gestalt.

SeverityRough markers
MildShort-acting bronchodilators only
ModerateSABA + steroids ± antibiotics
SevereED/hospital; possible respiratory failure
Admit for: marked symptom increase (rest dyspnea), severe underlying COPD, failure of outpatient therapy, new hypoxemia/hypercapnia, altered mentation, or inadequate home support.

2 · The core bundle

  • Bronchodilators: short-acting beta-agonist ± short-acting antimuscarinic, scheduled + PRN (nebs or MDI-spacer equivalent).
  • Systemic steroids: prednisone 40 mg PO × 5 days (REDUCE) — oral equals IV; no taper needed for a 5-day course.
  • Antibiotics if ≥ 2 of: ↑dyspnea, ↑sputum volume, ↑sputum purulence (purulence is the key one) — or if mechanically ventilated. Usual 5 days; cover H. flu/pneumococcus/Moraxella (e.g., macrolide, doxycycline, or amox-clav); add Pseudomonas coverage if risk factors.
  • Oxygen: titrate to SpO2 88–92% — avoid hyperoxia, which worsens hypercapnia.
Over-oxygenating a CO2-retainer can precipitate narcosis (V/Q mismatch + Haldane effect + loss of hypoxic drive). Target 88–92%, not 100%.

3 · Escalate breathing — NIV

NIV (BiPAP) is the highest-impact intervention in acute hypercapnic respiratory failure — it lowers intubation rate and mortality.

GOLD 2026 also positions high-flow nasal cannula as a supportive option, but NIV remains first-line for acute hypercapnic acidosis. Get an early ABG/VBG to find the retainers.

Find the trigger / mimics

  • Most common: viral and bacterial respiratory infection; also air pollution, nonadherence.
  • Always exclude mimics: pneumonia (CXR), heart failure (BNP/exam), PE (consider in any unexplained exacerbation), pneumothorax, arrhythmia.
  • Work-up: CXR, ABG/VBG, ECG, BMP/CBC; viral PCR per season; sputum culture if Pseudomonas risk or vent.

Discharge to prevent the next one · Do/Avoid

  • DO confirm/optimize inhaler technique and step up maintenance: LAMA+LABA; add ICS if blood eosinophils elevated and ≥1 moderate/severe exacerbation (GOLD 2026).
  • DO offer smoking cessation, vaccines (influenza, pneumococcal, COVID, RSV), and pulmonary rehab referral within 4 weeks (mortality benefit).
  • DO arrange early follow-up and a written action plan; assess home O2 need after recovery (not at discharge).
  • AVOID antibiotics when purulence is absent and not ventilated
  • AVOID long steroid courses/tapers — 5 days is enough (REDUCE)
  • AVOID oxygen targets of 94–100% in COPD
