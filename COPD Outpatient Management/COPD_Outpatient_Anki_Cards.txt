What is mandatory to diagnose COPD?	Post-bronchodilator spirometry showing FEV1/FVC <0.70 in a patient with symptoms/risk factors. You cannot diagnose COPD without PFTs.
Classic spirometry/PFT findings in COPD?	Scooped (concave) expiratory flow-volume loop, FEV1/FVC <0.7; supportive: ↑TLC and ↑RV (air trapping), ↓DLCO (emphysema). Post-bronchodilator FEV1 grades severity.
What are pre-COPD and PRISm?	Pre-COPD = respiratory symptoms or structural lesions (e.g., emphysema) WITHOUT airflow obstruction (FEV1/FVC ≥0.7). PRISm = Preserved Ratio Impaired Spirometry. Both carry risk of progressing to COPD.
When should alpha-1 antitrypsin testing be done in COPD?	Once in ALL patients with COPD — especially if young (<45), panlobular basal emphysema, or a suggestive family history.
2023 GOLD ABE groups — how are they defined?	Group A: 0–1 exacerbations + low symptoms (mMRC 0–1 / CAT <10). Group B: 0–1 exacerbations + high symptoms (mMRC ≥2 / CAT ≥10). Group E: ≥2 moderate exacerbations or ≥1 requiring hospitalization.
First-line inhaler therapy by GOLD group (2023)?	Group A: a long-acting bronchodilator (LABA or LAMA). Groups B and E: LABA + LAMA. Initial triple (add ICS) in Group E if blood eos ≥300.
When is ICS added in COPD, and what is the key risk?	Add ICS (→ triple therapy) for ongoing exacerbations with blood eos ≥100 (initial ICS in Group E if eos ≥300). Key risk: pneumonia — de-escalate ICS if recurrent pneumonias.
LABA vs LAMA vs ICS roles in COPD?	LABA: symptom relief, less effect on exacerbations. LAMA: symptoms AND fewer exacerbations (LAMA > LABA for exacerbations). ICS: fewer exacerbations if eos elevated, but ↑ pneumonia risk.
Which COPD interventions reduce mortality?	Smoking cessation (clearest benefit); long-term oxygen in resting hypoxemia; and vaccinations. Pulmonary rehab improves symptoms and exacerbations; lung cancer screening reduces cancer mortality in eligible smokers.
Criteria for long-term home oxygen in COPD?	Resting PaO2 ≤55 mmHg (or SpO2 ≤88%), OR PaO2 56–59 mmHg with cor pulmonale / right heart failure / polycythemia.
Three cardinal symptoms of a COPD exacerbation?	Increased dyspnea, increased sputum volume, and increased sputum purulence.
Outpatient steroid regimen for a COPD exacerbation?	Prednisone 40 mg daily ×5 days (REDUCE trial: short course non-inferior to longer). Reduces relapse and symptoms.
When are antibiotics indicated in a COPD exacerbation, and for how long?	When ≥2 cardinal symptoms are present (must include increased sputum purulence) or the patient needs ventilation. ~5-day course; agent chosen by local resistance patterns.
Bronchodilator choice in an acute COPD exacerbation?	Short-acting: SABA ± SAMA. Metered-dose inhalers are as effective as nebulizers when used correctly.
When is NIPPV indicated in a COPD exacerbation?	Acidemic hypercapnic respiratory failure: pH <7.35 with PaCO2 >45 mmHg.
What did GOLD 2024/2025 change about ICS-containing therapy?	When ICS is indicated, triple therapy (LABA+LAMA+ICS) is preferred over LABA-ICS. Eosinophil thresholds for ICS (≥100 escalate, ≥300 initial in Group E) unchanged.
First biologic approved for COPD, and its niche?	Dupilumab (Dupixent) — add to triple therapy for ongoing exacerbations with blood eos ≥300 and chronic bronchitis. FDA-approved September 2024 (BOREAS/NOTUS).
Ensifentrine (Ohtuvayre) — what is it?	A novel inhaled dual PDE3/PDE4 inhibitor (bronchodilator + anti-inflammatory) by nebulizer; FDA-approved June 2024 (ENHANCE) — first new inhaled COPD mechanism in ~20 years.
