Chronic Heart Failure Management — Clinical One-Pager

Classification · HFrEF 4-pillar GDMT · titration · EF spectrum. Core per 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA. Adapted from TeachIM.org (CC BY-NC 4.0). Educational use only.

1 · The HF one-liner

NYHA (I asx · II sx w/ normal activity · III sx w/ less-than-ordinary · IV at rest/ADLs) + EF + etiology (ischemic ~50-60% vs non-ischemic: HTN, EtOH, tachy-induced, infiltrative, genetic).
EF: HFrEF ≤40 · HFmrEF 41-49 · HFpEF ≥50 · HFimpEF = was ≤40, improved ≥10 pts to >40.

~73% combined mortality reduction at target doses; only ~1% reach them (CHAMP-HF). Add/titrate something every visit (q1-2 wk; STRONG-HF).

When to start each pillar

  • BB: only when compensated (euvolemic).
  • ARNI: SBP>100, 36h off ACEi (ARB→immediate); can start inpatient.
  • ACEi/ARB: stable Cr & K.
  • MRA: hold if K>5, GFR<30, Cr>2.5 M />2 F.
  • SGLT2i: avoid GFR<20, type 1 DM / DKA hx.

Roadblocks — keep GDMT going

AKI: a <30% GFR dip on ACEi/ARNI + SGLT2i is expected & OK; fix volume/diuretic first, hold ACEi/ARNI temporarily, re-trial after resolution.
Low BP: treat symptoms not the number (asx SBP 90-100 OK); drop non-GDMT antiHTN (CCB/thiazide); stagger dosing; carvedilol→metop succinate.
HyperK: SGLT2i & ARNI mitigate; low-K diet; hydralazine/isosorbide as last resort.

Device / vasodilator (EF<35%, ≥3 mo GDMT)

  • CRT if QRS >120-150 ms, esp. LBBB — NNT 8
  • ICD if life expectancy >1 yr (CRT-D combines both) — NNT 23
  • Hydralazine + isosorbide: self-ID Black pt on GDMT (Class 1a, A-HeFT) or when ACEi/ARB/ARNI not tolerated
