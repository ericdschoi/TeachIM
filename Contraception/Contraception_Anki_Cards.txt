What do the PATH questions assess?	Pregnancy Attitudes, Timing, and How important is prevention — patient-centered openers that don't assume a "plan."
Default starting combined OCP?	~20 mcg ethinyl estradiol + a progestin; pick 2-3 favorites and titrate.
Estrogen dose tradeoff in OCPs?	Lower estrogen = more breakthrough bleeding; higher estrogen = more thrombosis.
Which progestin generations are more anti-androgenic, and the catch?	3rd/4th generation; but higher VTE risk when combined with estrogen.
Four history items that exclude combined (estrogen) methods?	Smoking at age >35, migraine with aura, VTE/CVD/CVA, breast cancer (breast CA excludes all hormonal methods).
US MEC categories 1-4?	1 = use without restriction; 2 = advantages > risks; 3 = risks usually outweigh advantages; 4 = do not use.
Combined method + smoking + age >=35 + >=15 cigarettes/day = which MEC category?	Category 4.
Lamotrigine + combined hormonal contraception interaction and category?	Category 3 — estrogen increases lamotrigine clearance, lowering levels (risk of breakthrough symptoms).
Most effective emergency contraception?	Copper IUD (>99%, up to 5 days, BMI-independent).
Ulipristal vs levonorgestrel for emergency contraception?	Ulipristal (Ella) is more effective, especially days 3-5 and higher BMI; both lose efficacy as BMI rises.
Yuzpe method for emergency contraception?	Combined OCPs: 100 mcg ethinyl estradiol + 0.5 mg levonorgestrel per dose (~4-5 pills), q12h x2 — least effective but most available.
Bleeding effect: copper IUD vs LNG IUD/implant?	Copper -> heavier/crampier; LNG IUD & implant -> lighter/amenorrhea (may have prolonged spotting early).
DMPA (Depo-Provera) bleeding pattern over time?	Lighter to amenorrhea, especially after >1 year; can have ongoing spotting.
How long until a method's bleeding pattern settles?	3-4 cycles; estrogen-free methods may spot 6-12 months, combined methods usually settle by ~3 months.
Most effective reversible method by typical use?	The etonogestrel implant (~0.05 pregnancies per 100 users/yr), then LNG and copper IUDs.
Combined hormonal method after major surgery with immobilization?	US MEC category 4 — switch to a progestin-only method.
Does testosterone prevent pregnancy (CDC 2024)?	No — a substantial fraction of users ovulate; offer contraception to testosterone users with a uterus who don't want pregnancy.
First OTC daily oral contraceptive?	Opill (norgestrel 0.075 mg, progestin-only) — FDA-approved July 2023, on shelves 2024.
US SPR 2024 tip to reduce IUD-placement pain?	Lidocaine — paracervical block or topical.
