Management of Decompensated Cirrhosis — Clinical One-Pager

Facilitator quick reference · TeachIM (Zhang/Fainstad, Jan 2020), optimized Jun 2026 · CC BY-NC 4.0 · clinician-review before use
Cirrhosis one-liner: cause | compensated vs DECOMPENSATED | MELD 3.0 | prior complications. Decompensation = ↑MELD or new complication. Precipitants overlap: infection/SBP, GI bleed, PVT.

Staging & MELD 3.0

Cause: EtOH 60–70%, MASH 10%, HCV/HBV 10%, hemochromatosis, AIH/PSC/PBC.

Compensated median survival >12 y; decompensated much shorter. MELD 3.0 (Cr/Tbili/INR/Na/albumin/sex) = US allocation (replaced MELD-Na, 2023).

Work-up of Acute Decompensation

  • Diagnostic paracentesis (SAAG, cell count, culture) — everyone admitted with ascites.
  • Evaluate for GI bleeding.
  • RUQ US with Doppler (PVT, HCC).
  • Recheck MELD components (Cr, Tbili, INR, Na).

2021–2023 Updates

  • MELD 3.0 (2023) replaced MELD-Na (adds albumin + sex).
  • Terlipressin FDA-approved 2022, first-line for HRS-AKI (type 1 HRS = HRS-AKI).
  • Baveno VII: carvedilol in compensated cirrhosis + CSPH prevents 1st decompensation.
  • NASH → MASH; NAFLD → MASLD (2023).

Pearls

  • Missed SBP is a preventable death — tap the belly.
  • Albumin in SBP reduces HRS & mortality (esp. Cr >1 or Tbili >4).
  • In variceal bleed, ceftriaxone is a survival drug — not optional.
  • Restrictive transfusion (Hgb ~7) beats liberal in UGIB.
  • Decompensation = put transplant evaluation on the problem list.
