Inpatient Hyperkalemia — 5-Step Framework

Confirm → ECG → Stabilize → Shift → Eliminate · Any K >5.5 gets a STAT ECG; treat K >6.5 as emergent regardless of ECG · complications: severe weakness, fatal arrhythmias

1 · Confirm — without delaying treatment

  • Expected? (AKI/CKD/ESRD, RASi, K-sparing diuretics, TMP-SMX, rhabdo, TLS, acidosis)
  • Hemolysis, fist clenching, poor collection → spurious K
  • Symptoms: myalgias, weakness, palpitations

2 · STAT ECG — progressive changes

EarlyPeaked T waves, QT shortening
WorsePR prolongs → P flattens/lost; QRS widens
Pre-arrestSine wave, VT/VF, asystole; pseudo-RBBB / pseudo-STE V1–V2
  • Repeat ECG q30–60 min until normal; telemetry
  • ECG changes predict outcome better than the K level, but correlate poorly with it; rapid rise >> chronic elevation for arrhythmia risk

3 · Stabilize myocardium — ECG changes or K >6.5

Ca gluconateCa chloride
Dose1–2 g IV, peripheral OK (preferred)0.5–1 g IV, central line (caustic)
KineticsOnset 3–5 min · lasts 30–60 min
EffectNo change in serum K — should normalize ECG; re-dose q5–10 min if changes persist

4 · Shift K into cells — temporizing only

AgentDoseOnset/lastsΔK (mEq/L)
Regular insulin + D5010U IV + amp D50; 5U if renal impairment; no D50 if BG>25015–60 min / 4–6 h↓0.5–1.0
Albuterol neb10–20 mg in 4 mL NS (>> 2.5 mg asthma dose)15–30 min / 2–4 h↓0.5–1.5
NaHCO350–100 mEq IV — only metabolic acidosis, not ESRD15–30 min / 1–2 hleast effective
  • Monitor glucose ~6 h post insulin (hypoglycemia = most common complication)
  • Albuterol: caution active CAD; best with insulin
  • Pick cocktail by comorbidity; shifting never removes K

5 · Eliminate K — kidney ~90% / gut ~10%

TherapyUse
Furosemide 40–160 mg IV1st-line if hypervolemic; + isotonic IVF if euvolemic; useless in anuria; alone can volume-deplete → worse AKI
HD / CRRTSevere renal dysfunction or refractory — most likely to normalize K within 4 h
SZC (Lokelma) 10 g TID ×48 hOnset ~1 h, preferred binder; Na load/edema, cost. UKKA 2023: option alongside shifting in life-threatening hyperK (K≥6.5)
PatiromerColon only, onset ~7 h — chronic niche
Kayexalate (SPS) 15–60 g q6h PO / 30–50 g PRAvoid: post-op, ileus/SBO, IBD, infectious colitis (bowel necrosis); separate other meds 3 h; skip if HD planned. UKKA 2023: not for emergency tx

Do / AVOID

  • DO recheck K in 1–4 h; repeat shift + eliminate until K normal or HD
  • DO (KDIGO 2024) lower K with diet review/diuretic/binder rather than stopping ACEi/ARB — RASi d/c linked to ↑mortality/CV events in CKD
  • AVOID NaHCO3 in ESRD (HD pts were the non-responders) or without acidosis
  • AVOID Kayexalate with any bowel risk or imminent HD
  • AVOID judging calcium by the K level — its endpoint is the ECG
Responders to bicarbonate in studies: K>6, HCO3<17, pH<7.35, not on HD (Abuelo, KI Rep 2018).
