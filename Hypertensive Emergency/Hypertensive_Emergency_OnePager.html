HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY — Clinical One-Pager

Severe HTN (>180/120) with acute end-organ damage · 2017 & 2025 AHA/ACC; AHA/ASA stroke · IM resident reference

1 · DEFINE

HTN emergency = SBP >180 or DBP >120 + acute end-organ damage. The damage defines it, not the number. Rate of rise > absolute value — chronic HTN tolerates very high BP; a previously normotensive pt (eclampsia, acute GN) damages below 180/120.
2025 AHA/ACC: "hypertensive urgency" retired → severe HTN without acute target-organ damage. Mgmt unchanged: reinstitute/uptitrate oral meds, outpatient, close f/u. No IV drip, no rapid drop.

2 · END-ORGAN SCREEN

SystemEmergencies / clues
NeuroHTN encephalopathy (insidious HA/confusion/szr; PRES = parieto-occipital edema); ICH/SAH & ischemic CVA (focal deficits); papilledema
CardiacAcute LV failure + pulm edema; ACS; aortic dissection (tearing pain to back, inter-arm BP diff, ↓pulses, widened mediastinum)
RenalRapidly ↑Cr, ↓UOP, microscopic hematuria
OtherEclampsia (>20 wk + szr); pheo crisis (episodic HA/sweats/palpitations/tachy/hyperthermia); sympathomimetics (+ tox); VEGF inhibitors; TMA

3 · TREATMENT GOALS

Goal = prevent further damage, NOT normalize BP. ICU + IV titratable gtt; remove exacerbators (pain, volume, drugs). Autoregulation has reset → fast drop = hypoperfusion.
ScenarioTarget / pace
General↓SBP ≤25% in 1st h → ~160/100 by 2–6 h → nl over 24–48 h
DissectionHR ~60 w/ BB first, then SBP <120 ASAP
ICH / SAHSBP <140 promptly
Eclampsia / pheoSBP <140 in 1st h (+ MgSO4 for eclampsia)
Ischemic CVAPermissive ≤220/120; if higher ↓~15%/24h; tPA cand <185/110 pre, <180/105 ×24h
Dissection: rate before pressure — vasodilator first triggers reflex tachycardia → ↑wall shear (dP/dt) → extends dissection.

SCENARIO → AGENT (quick pick)

  • Ischemic stroke / pregnancy: nicardipine
  • ACS / pulmonary edema: nitroglycerin ± nitroprusside + diuresis
  • Aortic dissection: esmolol/labetalol (HR 60) then nitroprusside
  • Pheo / sympathomimetic: phentolamine (then maybe nicardipine/nitroprusside)
  • Acute renal failure: fenoldopam or nicardipine

DO / AVOID

AVOID: β-blocker before EF known in acute LV failure (→ cardiogenic shock); β-blocker as initial Rx in pheo/catecholamine excess (unopposed α); nitroprusside in AKI (cyanide); hydralazine in ACS (reflex tachy); rapid normalization in any emergency.
DO: confirm organ damage before calling it an emergency; lower gradually to target; treat the driver (pain, missed meds, tox, eclampsia); ICU + a-line for any gtt.
