Hyponatremia — the 3-Lab Diagnostic Approach

Serum osm "is it real?" → urine osm "is ADH on?" → urine Na "are the kidneys seeing enough fluid?" · skip the unreliable bedside volume guess · diagnosis only — treatment is a separate pathway

1 · Serum osm — is it real?

SOsmReadCauses / action
<290True hypo-osmolar hypoNa→ Step 2
NormalPseudohyponatremia↑TG, ↑protein (myeloma) dilute whole plasma → send whole-blood (direct ISE) Na
>290HyperosmolarActive osmoles (glucose, mannitol, sorbitol) pull water from cells; inactive (BUN, EtOH) raise osm but do NOT cause hypoNa
Corrected Na = measured + 1.6 × (glucose − 100)/100 — glc 500 hides ~6.5 mEq/L

2 · Urine osm — is ADH on or off?

UOsmReadDifferential
<100ADH OFF (kidney maximally diluting — intake is the problem)Primary polydipsia · beer potomania · tea-and-toast (low solute)
>100ADH ONEverything else → Step 3: is the ADH appropriate?
  • ADH (pituitary) fires on low volume/ECV (baroreceptors) or high SOsm → collecting duct reclaims water → concentrated urine

3 · Urine Na — do the kidneys see enough fluid?

UNaPhysiologyDifferential
<25RAAS on → Na reclaimed; ADH appropriateTrue hypovolemia (GI loss, bleeding) · low ECV w/ high total volume: HF, cirrhosis
>40Kidneys perfused → ADH inappropriateSIADH · hypothyroidism · adrenal insufficiency · diuretics (exception: salt wasting falsely ↑UNa)
  • SIADH triggers: antidepressants/antiepileptics/antipsychotics, surgery, pain, CNS injury, lung disease, malignancy — patient euvolemic
  • Cerebral salt wasting (esp. SAH): SIADH-pattern labs but HYPOVOLEMIC
  • Always check TSH + cortisol before calling SIADH — replacement corrects both impostors

Worked cases (Na 125 in all three)

LabsDiagnosis
SOsm 270 / UOsm 60 / UNa 65 — drinkerBeer potomania (ADH off, low solute)
SOsm 310 / UOsm 800 / UNa 20 — glc 500, DKAHyperosmolar hypoNa (corrected ~131) + hypovolemia
SOsm 265 / UOsm 400 / UNa <10 — vomiting + diarrheaTrue hypovolemic hypoNa (appropriate ADH)

Do / AVOID

  • DO confirm hypo-osmolality before any further workup — artifact and glucose first
  • DO read UOsm before UNa: a dilute urine ends the workup (intake problem)
  • DO check TSH + AM cortisol before diagnosing SIADH
  • AVOID interpreting urine Na on diuretics — salt wasting voids the rule
  • AVOID leading with a bedside volume exam — labs first, exam to break SIADH-vs-CSW ties
  • AVOID rapid correction of chronic hypoNa (ODS risk) — treatment is its own pathway
Framework unchanged in current guidance — 2024 Austrian Society for Nephrology consensus uses the same SOsm/UOsm/UNa triad.
