Inflammatory Arthritis — Pattern-Based Approach

Place every inflammatory joint complaint on a 2-axis grid: TIME (acute vs chronic) × SPACE (mono = 1 · oligo = 2–4 · poly = 5+) — then let synovial fluid arbitrate.

The Time-and-Space Grid

Mono / OligoPoly
AcuteSeptic, gout, CPPD, Lyme (early), GCGC, reactive, viral, sarcoid (Löfgren's)
ChronicFungal/TB, Lyme, tophaceous goutRA, SLE, psoriatic, ankylosing spondylitis
Acute monoarthritis = septic until proven otherwise. Tap before steroids. Crystals do NOT exclude infection.

Gout ULT Rules (ACR 2020)

  • Start ULT: ≥2 flares/yr, tophi, or erosions; consider after 1 flare if CKD ≥3, urate >9, or urolithiasis.
  • Allopurinol first-line (incl. CKD) — start low, titrate. Goal urate <6 (<5 to shrink tophi).
  • Keep prophylaxis (low-dose colchicine) while titrating — prevents mobilization flares.
  • Tophaceous: punched-out erosions, overhanging edges; favors cool sites (fingertips, toes, Achilles, elbows).

Screen the whole patient: rash, eye disease (uveitis), IBD/GI, GU symptoms, enthesitis, dactylitis — the extra-articular ROS is what separates the spondyloarthropathies.

Do / AVOID

  • DO tap every acute monoarthritis before steroids; blood cultures if febrile.
  • DO image: affected joint ± SI films if axial symptoms; hands/feet q1–2 yr in RA.
  • AVOID ANA/RF to work up acute arthritis — viruses cause transient autoantibodies.
  • AVOID ruling gout in/out by serum urate alone — may be normal during flares.
  • Red flags → urgent action: fever + monoarthritis, prosthetic joint, IVDU, immunosuppression → tap + empiric abx + ortho; chronic >6 wk or erosions → rheumatology.
