Resp + cardiac are most common. Toolbox: CXR, blood gas, CBC, ECG + POCUS.

A-a gradient

PAO2 = FiO2×(Patm−PH2O) − PaCO2/0.8 ≈ 150 − 1.25×PaCO2 (room air)

A-a = PAO2 − PaO2; expected = age/4 + 4 (64 yo → ~20)

Worked: PaO2 50, PaCO2 32 → PAO2 110; A-a = 60 (elevated vs expected 20).

Low FiO2NormalAltitude; not acute
HypoventilationNormal↑PaCO2; opioids/CNS/NM weakness
V/Q mismatch↑Most common; PE, obstruction
Shunt↑No correction w/ 100% O2
↓ Diffusion↑Chronic (ILD, emphysema)

Algorithm: normal A-a → hypoventilation (check PaCO2); elevated A-a → V/Q vs shunt (100% O2 challenge).

ABG vs VBG

Get an ABG when you need the PaO2: confirm/grade hypoxemia or compute the A-a gradient. Else VBG suffices.

ABG pH ≈ VBG pH + 0.04; ABG PaCO2 ≈ VBG PvCO2 − 4.

Hypoxia that isn't hypoxemia (normal PaO2)

  • Anemic; stagnant (low flow / cardiac)
  • Histotoxic — cyanide (sodium nitroprusside)
  • Methemoglobinemia — nitrates, local anesthetics, sulfonamides
  • SpO2 falsely reassuring in CO poisoning / MetHb → confirm with co-oximetry

POCUS / pearls

  • BLUE-protocol lung US: B-lines (edema), consolidation (PNA), absent sliding (PTX), effusion — high sensitivity
  • Dyspnea ≠ hypoxemia; normal PaO2 75–100
  • Shunt = perfusion without ventilation; fails the 100% O2 challenge
  • Tamponade clues: hypotension, ↑JVP, electrical alternans, enlarged cardiac silhouette
