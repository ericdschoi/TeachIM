Is It Lupus? — ANA Stewardship & SLE Workup

Decide whether to test BEFORE you send an ANA. Each feature counts only if unexplained (EULAR/ACR 2019).

Why SLE Looks Like Everything

  • UV → apoptosis → macrophage uptake of self-antigen → two pathways:
  • Antibody: APC → T-helper → B cell → ANA → cell destruction, immune-complex deposition, + ANA test.
  • Inflammatory: antigen → TLR → IL-1, IL-6, IFN-α → inflammation anywhere.
  • Autoantibodies brew for years (up to ~9.4 yr) before clinical disease.

Diffuse pain + normal exam? Think fibromyalgia / thyroid / OA / spondyloarthritis — not lupus. No red/hot/swollen joint and normal markers argue against SLE.

If Testing Is Indicated — Send the Panel

  • CBC w/ diff (cytopenias; lympho/neutropenia common) · CMP (↑Cr=LN, transaminitis=AIH, ↑bili=hemolysis)
  • C3/C4 (low expected) · ESR/CRP (ESR » CRP; high CRP → infection or RA overlap)
  • UA (hematuria + proteinuria = lupus nephritis; up to 50% of SLE)

ANA Stewardship & Pearls

  • Positive ANA in healthy people: ~32% at 1:40, ~13% at 1:80, ~5% at 1:160 — order for probability, not reassurance.
  • Known SLE + high CRP → infection first (leading cause of mortality; ESR » CRP in pure flare).
  • ≥20% of SLE patients also have fibromyalgia — it explains many overlapping symptoms.
  • Refer rheumatology when abnormal labs accompany unexplained SLE-specific features; urgent if end-organ damage, severe cytopenia, extensive/bullous rash, or pregnancy.
Treatment (out of scope, 2024 ACR LN): early combination therapy — HCQ for all + steroids + (MPAA+belimumab) or (MPAA+voclosporin) or Euro-Lupus CYC→MPAA+belimumab; taper steroids to ≤5 mg/d by 6 mo. Refer for management.
