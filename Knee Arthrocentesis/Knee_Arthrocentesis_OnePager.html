KNEE ARTHROCENTESIS & INJECTION — One-Pager

Superolateral intra-articular approach + knee exam · TeachIM simulation curriculum · IM resident reference

1 · INDICATIONS

  • Symptomatic knee OA; aseptic inflammatory arthritis flare
  • Diagnostic aspiration: evaluate effusion, r/o septic joint / crystals

2 · CONTRAINDICATIONS

AbsoluteHardware/prosthesis in joint; overlying skin infection; suspected septic joint (aspirate & treat, do NOT inject steroid)
NOT contraindic.Anticoagulation; well-controlled DM; surgery >3 mo out (defer steroid if <3 mo)

Pes anserine bursitis: point TTP anteromedial tibia ~2–5 cm BELOW the joint line (anterior & inferior to it) — distinguishes from medial meniscus tear.

4 · WHAT WORKS (ACR 2019)

  • Core: exercise/PT + weight loss — more effective long-term than injection
  • First-line drug: topical NSAID (diclofenac), oral NSAID
  • Steroid injection: small-mod relief ~6 wk (Cochrane 2015) — for flares/short-term
  • ACR recommends AGAINST hyaluronic acid, PRP, stem cells
Don't over-inject: repeated IA steroids may accelerate cartilage/structural loss (McAlindon JAMA 2017; OAI 2019).

5 · TECHNIQUE (superolateral)

  • Position: supine, leg fully extended (relaxes quad/patellar tendon).
  • Mark superior + lateral patellar edges → insertion point superolateral to patella.
  • Chlorhexidine scrub ×30 s. Displace patella laterally.
  • Needle parallel to floor, slightly under patella (perpendicular to leg or slightly toward toes), ~3 cm.
  • Aspirate first (no blood) → inject lido + triamcinolone 40 mg WITHOUT resistance.
Resistance = NOT in joint space → withdraw & redirect. Free flow = intra-articular.

6 · COUNSELING

  • Diabetics: glucose may rise >100 mg/dL for ~24–48 h (up to several days), worse with poor baseline control.
  • Refer PT; start within ~4 wk for durable benefit.
  • Return precautions: signs of joint infection.

MENISCAL TEAR: PT vs SURGERY

  • Degenerative tear (older + OA): PT = arthroscopy for pain/function (Khan CMAJ 2014); MRI rarely changes mgmt.
  • Surgery favored: younger, acute tear, true mechanical sx (locking / can't extend).

SUPPLIES

Marker · chlorhexidine · 1% lido (5 cc) · triamcinolone 40 mg/mL (1 cc) · 18 g (draw-up) + 21–25 g 1.5" (inject) · 5 cc syringe · gauze · band-aid (+ 18 g & 20–30 cc syringe if aspirating).
