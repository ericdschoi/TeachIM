Indication for a therapeutic (large-volume) paracentesis?	Symptomatic ascites: abdominal distention/pain, early satiety, or dyspnea from increased intra-abdominal pressure. (Large ascites alone, asymptomatic, is not by itself an indication.)
When is a diagnostic paracentesis indicated?	New-onset ascites; and EVERY cirrhotic admitted to hospital with ascites (to exclude SBP), even if asymptomatic.
What defers a LARGE-VOLUME tap (vs a diagnostic tap)?	Hemodynamic instability / sepsis (large-volume removal worsens circulatory dysfunction); surgical abdomen; DIC/clinically evident fibrinolysis. A diagnostic tap is still indicated in sepsis to evaluate for SBP.
Are INR >2 or platelets <50K contraindications to paracentesis?	No, coagulopathy alone does not increase clinically significant bleeding; AASLD does not recommend routine FFP/platelet transfusion before the tap.
Is intra-abdominal hypertension a contraindication to paracentesis?	No, it is relieved BY the paracentesis.
Minimum "safe pocket" on ultrasound for a tap?	More than 2-3 cm of hypoechoic fluid (peritoneum to viscera) in all 3 dimensions. For a worthwhile therapeutic tap, want maximal depth >5 cm.
Which ultrasound transducer to evaluate ascites depth?	Low-frequency CURVILINEAR (or phased-array). Use the high-frequency LINEAR probe + Doppler for the vascular screen.
Insertion landmark for paracentesis?	LLQ (or RLQ) ~2 cm superior and ~2 cm medial to the anterior superior iliac spine, avoids flank musculature, stays lateral to the rectus sheath.
Two vessels to screen for with Doppler before the tap?	Superficial abdominal-wall veins at the site (compress with light pressure) and the inferior epigastric vessels (just lateral to the rectus abdominis, stay lateral to them).
Why avoid a too-medial puncture in paracentesis?	The inferior epigastric arteries run just lateral to the rectus sheath; laceration causes a rectus-sheath hematoma.
Patient positioning for paracentesis?	HOB 15-45 deg, rotate ~15 deg toward the side of the tap so bowel floats up and fluid pools dependently. Matters most late in the drain as the space shrinks.
Sterile gear for paracentesis vs CVC?	Mask, eye protection, sterile gloves (cap/gown optional), less than the maximal sterile barrier required for a CVC. Chlorhexidine scrub x2, 30 s each.
Which drainage tubing for paracentesis?	Simple IV tubing with NO one-way valves, to a vacuum bottle/vacutainer (different from the valved thoracentesis set).
Albumin replacement after large-volume paracentesis?	6-8 g of 25% albumin per liter removed for taps >5 L (~75 g for 10 L). Prevents post-paracentesis circulatory dysfunction; improves survival (AASLD 2021). Not needed for <4-5 L.
Troubleshooting when ascites flow slows/stops?	(1) Turn off suction + reposition (raise HOB/roll to side); (2) flush catheter 5-10 cc saline (bowel may be plugging it); (3) rotate catheter; (4) withdraw to ~2 cm + skin-to-peritoneum depth (~4 cm). Too little fluid (<2 cm), use dynamic US or IR.
High SAAG (>=1.1) + HIGH ascitic protein (>2.5) suggests?	Cardiac / post-hepatic cause (e.g., heart failure), get an echocardiogram. Cirrhotic ascites is high-SAAG but LOW-protein.
SBP diagnosis on fluid cell count?	Ascitic PMN >=250 cells/mm3, treat (e.g., ceftriaxone). 40% of 420 = 168, NOT SBP.
MPS vs UPS thresholds on the paracentesis CAT?	MPS (graduate sim): <30/41 mistakes, Global >2, Entrustment >2 (Angoff-set). UPS (unsupervised practice): <8/41 mistakes.
