LARGE VOLUME PARACENTESIS — Procedure One-Pager

US-guided bedside ascites drainage · TeachIM simulation curriculum · IM resident reference

1 · INDICATIONS

Therapeutic (LVP)Symptomatic ascites: distention/pain, early satiety, dyspnea. (Asymptomatic large ascites alone is NOT an indication.)
DiagnosticNew-onset ascites; EVERY cirrhotic admitted with ascites (r/o SBP), even if asymptomatic

2 · CONTRAINDICATIONS

Defer LVPHemodynamic instability / sepsis (large-volume removal worsens circulatory dysfn); surgical abdomen; DIC / clinical fibrinolysis
NOT contraindic.INR >2, plt <50K (no routine FFP/plt); intra-abd HTN (relieved BY tap). Pregnancy/prior surgery = use US, avoid scars
Coags don't block the tap: paracentesis is safe across a wide INR/plt range; AASLD recommends against routine correction. It's the hemodynamics that defer a large-volume tap.

3 · US SITE SELECTION

  • Low-freq curvilinear/phased-array: safe pocket >2–3 cm (peritoneum→viscera, all 3 dim); >5 cm to justify a therapeutic tap. Note probe angle = needle angle.
  • Site: LLQ/RLQ ~2 cm sup + ~2 cm medial to ASIS, lateral to rectus.
  • High-freq linear + Doppler: screen superficial abd-wall veins (light pressure) + identify inferior epigastric vessels (lateral to rectus).
SAFETY: stay LATERAL to the rectus — the inferior epigastric arteries run just lateral to the rectus sheath; a too-medial stick → rectus-sheath hematoma.

4 · TECHNIQUE

  • Time-out w/ RN before opening sterile supplies.
  • Gear: mask, eye protn, sterile gloves (cap/gown optional). Chlorhexidine scrub ×2, 30 s (iodine = apply & dry).
  • Anesthesia: 1% lido wheal → 21 g under negative pressure to fluid → ~2 cc at peritoneum.
  • #11 nick → catheter-over-needle (Z-track) under negative pressure to fluid → advance 0.5 cm, thread catheter to hub, remove needle.
  • Drain: simple IV tubing (NO valves) to vacuum bottle; exchange until flow stops + symptoms improve.

5 · ALBUMIN

6–8 g of 25% albumin per liter removed for taps >5 L (~75 g for 10 L). Prevents post-paracentesis circulatory dysfunction; improves survival (AASLD 2021). Not needed for <4–5 L.

6 · FLUID STUDIES

SuspectSend
Every tapCell count + diff; on 1st tap albumin + total protein (SAAG)
Infection (SBP)Gram stain + culture (bedside into blood-cx bottles). PMN ≥250 = SBP → ceftriaxone
MalignancyCytology
High SAAG (≥1.1) + HIGH protein (>2.5) → cardiac/post-hepatic cause → echocardiogram (cirrhotic ascites is high-SAAG, LOW-protein).

FLOW STOPPED? — TROUBLESHOOT

  • Turn off suction → reposition (raise HOB / roll to side)
  • Flush catheter 5–10 cc saline (bowel plugging it)
  • Rotate catheter
  • Withdraw to ~2 cm + skin-to-peritoneum depth (~4 cm)
  • Too little fluid (<2 cm) → dynamic US or IR
