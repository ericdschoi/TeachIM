LUMBAR PUNCTURE — Procedure One-Pager

Bedside diagnostic/therapeutic LP · TeachIM simulation curriculum · IM resident reference

1 · INDICATIONS

Dx — urgentSuspected meningitis/encephalitis; SAH w/ negative CT
Dx — non-urgentCNS malignancy (cytology); NPH; autoimmune (MS, GBS, CNS vasculitis, paraneoplastic)
TherapeuticIIH/pseudotumor (also dx); intrathecal med or contrast

2 · CONTRAINDICATIONS

AbsoluteOverlying skin infection / suspected epidural abscess at site
RelativeSpace-occupying lesion / Arnold-Chiari (CT ± NSGY first) · plt <50K · INR ≥1.5 · therapeutic anticoag · prior lumbar surgery
CT before LP only if: AMS, focal deficit, papilledema, seizure <1 wk, or immunocompromise (Hasbun NEJM 2001). Never delay antibiotics for CT/LP in suspected bacterial meningitis — cultures + empiric abx first.

3 · POSITION & LANDMARKS

Sitting uprightLateral decubitus
Lean over table, feet on stool, knees > hips. Easier landmarks. No OP possible.Handed-side down; stack ankles/knees/shoulders; chin tucked, knees up. Required for OP.
  • Palpation: iliac crest → ~L4; mark midline at L3–L4, L4–L5, or L2–L3.
  • Stay L2–L5 (below conus medullaris).
  • US (recommended): low-freq probe from sacrum up; transverse = midline, longitudinal = interspaces. Improves success, fewer passes (SHM 2019).

4 · TECHNIQUE

  • Time-out w/ RN (name/DOB/procedure/site/allergies) before opening sterile supplies.
  • Prep: iodine-povidone ×3, let dry (preferred over CHG). Fenestrated drape landscape.
  • Anesthesia: 1% lidocaine w/o epi; dermal wheal ≤0.5 cc (don't obscure marks), then tract.
  • Needle: midline, toward umbilicus, stylet in; advance slowly, remove stylet frequently for CSF. Bone → withdraw to subQ, redirect midline.
SAFETY STOP: never inject through the spinal needle; never advance without the stylet. Can't stay midline → re-mark (US) & reposition, don't fan blindly.
Use an atraumatic (pencil-point) needle — strong rec for ALL patients; halves PDPH (~11%→~4%), equal success (BMJ/Lancet 2018). 22 g balances PDPH/flow/OP. Replace stylet before withdrawal. Bed rest & IV fluids do NOT prevent PDPH.

5 · OPENING PRESSURE & COLLECTION

  • OP (lateral decubitus only): normal 10–25 cm H₂O. Elevated → straighten legs (drops ~5 cm H₂O). Upright falsely elevates.
  • Volume: routine (chem, cell count, viral, bacterial cx) ~8–12 mL over 4 tubes; cytology + fungal/AFB ≥30 mL.
  • Slow flow: rotate needle in 30° increments.
  • Closure: replace stylet → withdraw needle (stylet in) → hemostasis → dressing.
