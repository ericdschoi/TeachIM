LUNG CANCER — Screening, Dx & First Steps

#1 cancer death worldwide · Stage I 5-yr ~73–90% vs Stage IV ~0–10% · USPSTF 2021 / ACS 2023 · IM resident reference

1 · WHO TO SCREEN (asymptomatic)

GuidelineAnnual low-dose CT if…
USPSTF 2021 (coverage)Age 50–80 · ≥20 pack-yr · current smoker OR quit ≤15 yr ("A-50-80-20-15")
ACS 2023 (expanded)Age 50–80 · ≥20 pack-yr · current OR former — no quit-time limit
Not eligible → reassess risk annually + smoking cessation (highest-yield). Other RFs: radon, occupational/chemical, 2nd-hand smoke, FHx (1st-deg), COPD/pulm fibrosis, prior CA.

2 · SYMPTOMATIC PATHWAY

Sx: cough, hemoptysis, dyspnea, chest pain; advanced → wt loss, malaise, bone pain (mets), HA/seizure (brain).
  • CXR first. Not concerning → risk-assess (hemoptysis/persistent sx still → CT). Concerning → CT chest ± A/P (contrast for nodes).
CT red flags: >15 mm · irregular/spiculated · thick-walled cavity · solid component in GGO · extrapulmonary lesions · interval growth.
Tissue is the issue. Core/excisional > FNA (preserves tissue for molecular).
