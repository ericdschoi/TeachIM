Menopause — Diagnosis & Management
Clinical diagnosis · symptom management (hormonal & nonhormonal) · MHT risk/benefit counseling. IM facilitator reference.

Definitions & Diagnosis

  • Perimenopause: irregular menses (changed interval) + hormonal symptoms; begins ~4 yr before LMP.
  • Menopause: 12 mo amenorrhea (retrospective); mean age ~51.
  • Clinical diagnosis — no testing if age >45 with classic symptoms. FSH is a poor single marker (SWAN).
  • Test if <45: hCG, TSH, prolactin ± FSH. <40 = full amenorrhea work-up (primary ovarian insufficiency).

Symptom Management

Avoid fluoxetine, sertraline for hot flashes (no benefit).

Menopausal Hormone Therapy (MHT)

  • Indication: moderate–severe hot flashes. Candidate: <60 yr OR <10 yr post-menopause (low absolute risk).
  • Add progesterone for any patient with an intact uterus (prevents endometrial hyperplasia).
  • Transdermal estrogen < oral for VTE/stroke risk → preferred for moderate ASCVD risk & migraine.
  • Duration ~5–10 yr, re-evaluate yearly, stop by ~60. Rebound VMS common on stopping.
  • Contraindications: breast cancer, prior VTE/stroke/CHD, active liver disease, unexplained bleeding, high-risk endometrial cancer.

WHI & Risk/Benefit (ages 50–59, 5 yr/1000 women)

WHI (2002): excess CHD/stroke/VTE/breast cancer — but mean age 63, overstating risk for younger symptomatic women.

Estrogen-alone profile is more favorable than E+P. Average absolute risk is small (~3 extra/1000/5 yr).

Newer Context (cite before teaching)

  • 2022 Menopause Society (NAMS): HT benefits outweigh risks for healthy symptomatic women <60 / <10 yr post-menopause; most effective for VMS & GSM, prevents fracture.
  • Fezolinetant (Veozah, NK3 antagonist, FDA 2023) — monitor LFTs. Elinzanetant (Lynkuet, dual NK1/NK3, FDA 2025) — ~73% VMS reduction, improves sleep.

Prevention & Pitfalls

  • Estrogen loss → ↑ atherosclerosis & osteoporosis: lifestyle ± statin by ASCVD risk; calcium, vit D, exercise, stop smoking, ↓ EtOH.
  • Heavy bleeding in perimenopause → rule out malignancy: hCG, pelvic exam, pelvic US ± endometrial biopsy.
  • Contraception still needed in perimenopause: IUD to age 55, OCPs to age 51.
