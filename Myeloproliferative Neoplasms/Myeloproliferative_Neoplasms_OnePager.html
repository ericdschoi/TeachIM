THROMBOCYTOSIS & LEUKOCYTOSIS

Thrombocytosis: reactive [Fe-def anemia (often missed), infection/inflam, post-splenectomy] vs primary (MPN). Magnitude ≠ thrombosis risk; don't treat reactive.
Leukocytosis: leukemoid [infection, steroids, stress, tobacco] vs malignant. CML clues: left shift, basophilia/eosinophilia, low LAP, WBC >100k (leukostasis rare in CML).

TREATMENT PRINCIPLES

MPNApproach
ETPrevent thrombosis; risk by age >60/prior clot. Low: observe+ASA. High: +cytoreduction; anticoag if venous, ASA if arterial
PVPhlebotomy (Hct <45%) + low-dose ASA; +cytoreduction if high risk
PMFPrognosis-driven; HSCT = only cure; JAK inhibitor for sx
CMLTKI (highly effective); switch for resistance; HSCT rare
2022–25: PV → ropeginterferon (Besremi) NCCN 1st-line cytoreductive. MF JAK inhibitors by counts: ruxolitinib, fedratinib, pacritinib (plt<50k), momelotinib (anemia). CML → asciminib (STAMP inhibitor; ≥2 prior TKIs or T315I).
