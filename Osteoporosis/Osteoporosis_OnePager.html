Osteoporosis — Clinical One-Pager

Screen · diagnose by 3 criteria · bisphosphonate first-line · monitor & holiday. 2023 ACP living guideline. Adapted from TeachIM.org (CC BY-NC 4.0) — clinician-reviewed before use.

Diagnosis (any of 3)

  • DXA T-score ≤ −2.5
  • Fragility fracture — low-trauma fx of hip, vertebra, wrist, pelvis, ribs, humerus (dx at any T-score; treat without waiting for DXA)
  • High FRAX — 10-yr ≥20% major OR ≥3% hip
ClassificationT-score
Normal> −1.0
Osteopenia−1.0 to −2.5
Osteoporosis< −2.5

Most fractures occur above a −2.5 T-score — use clinical risk, not BMD alone.

Screening — who

  • All women ≥65; postmenopausal women <65 with ≥1 risk factor (use FRAX); men with major risk factors.
  • Risk factors: glucocorticoids (≥20 mg ×3 mo), aromatase inhibitors, ADT, antiepileptics; tobacco, alcohol >2/d; premature menopause, hypogonadism, hyperparathyroidism, T1DM, Cushing; celiac/IBD, RA, CLD, CKD, HIV, myeloma; low BMI, FHx hip fx, prior fx.

Secondary work-up

Indicated in all men, premenopausal women, or unexplained/<50.

  • Initial: CBC, CMP, Mg, Phos, 25-OH vit D, testosterone (men).
  • Directed: TSH, intact PTH, celiac panel, SPEP/UPEP, AM cortisol, FSH/LH/estradiol.

Primary interventions (ALL)

  • Calcium ~1200 mg/d (diet preferred); vitamin D 800 IU/d (50,000 IU/wk if <40).
  • Weight-bearing high-impact exercise; smoking cessation; limit alcohol.
  • VITAL (NEJM 2022): vit D alone did not cut fractures — but replete when starting a bisphosphonate; don't delay treatment.

Targeted therapy by risk

AgentRole / notes
Bisphosphonate (alendronate PO wkly; zoledronic IV yrly)1st-line, eGFR ≥35. PO: empty stomach, 8 oz water, upright ≥30 min.
Denosumab (SQ q6 mo)2nd-line (intol/eGFR <35). Never stop without bridging to a bisphosphonate → rebound vert fx; do NOT follow with PTH analog.
Anabolic (teriparatide SQ qd; romosozumab SQ qmo)Very-high-risk (T ≤−3.5, or ≤−2.5 + fx). Anabolic FIRST, then antiresorptive. Avoid romo if recent MI/CVA.
SERM (raloxifene)Rare; selected postmenopausal (also ↑ breast-ca risk). Harms: VTE, hot flashes.

Harms to counsel: esophagitis, hypocalcemia; rare ONJ & atypical femur fracture.

Monitoring & drug holiday

SituationRepeat DXA
Low risk (normal)~15 yr
Moderate (osteopenia)1–2 yr
On IV bisphosphonate~3 yr
On oral bisphosphonate~5 yr
During drug holiday2–4 yr

Drug holiday after 5 yr PO / 3 yr IV if BMD stable/improved & risk fallen. If worse / still high-risk → continue or refer endo (denosumab/anabolic).

2023–24 updates

  • ACP: bisphosphonate 1st-line (strong in women); denosumab 2nd-line.
  • Very-high-risk women: anabolic-first then antiresorptive (greater/earlier benefit).
  • ASBMR/BHOF 2024: treat-to-target (goal BMD), not fixed duration.
