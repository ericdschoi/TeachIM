Polycystic Ovary Syndrome — Clinical Reference

Endocrine disorder, 8–13% of reproductive-age people with ovaries · TeachIM.org (May 2022, optimized Jun 2026) · CC BY-NC 4.0 · educational use only — clinician-review before teaching

1 · Diagnosis — Rotterdam (2 of 3) + exclusion

CriterionDefinition
HyperandrogenismClinical (hirsutism, acne, androgenic alopecia) OR biochemical (↑ androgens). Serum misses 20–40%
Ovulatory dysfunctionOligomenorrhea (<8 cyc/yr or >35 d) or amenorrhea (>3 mo)
Polycystic ovaries>12 follicles and/or volume >10 cc on US (or AMH in adults — 2023)
  • Diagnosis of EXCLUSION — confirm only after ruling out mimics. Hirsutism = androgen-pattern terminal hair; acne/alopecia alone unreliable. Voice deepening/clitoromegaly → think tumor.

2 · Workup (exclude mimics)

FeatureMimicsTests
HyperandrogenismNon-classic CAH, acromegaly, androgen tumor, Cushing'sTotal testosterone; ±17-OHP; ±SHBG, DHEAS; midnight salivary cortisol if Cushingoid
Menstrual irregularityPregnancy, thyroid, prolactinoma, POI, structuralTSH±fT4, β-hCG, prolactin; ±pelvic US; ±LH/FSH
  • Total testosterone is the single best androgen test (free T unreliable). ↑ LH/FSH supports PCOS; its absence doesn't exclude it.

3 · Comorbidity screening (ALL patients)

DomainScreen → manage
Insulin resistance / T2DM (7.5–10× risk)A1c / fasting glucose / 2-h OGTT q1–2y → lifestyle ± metformin
Cardiovascular (↑LDL, ↓HDL)BP + fasting lipids → statin per risk
Mood / eating (depression, anxiety, binge)PHQ-9, GAD-7 → refer
AlsoOSA, NAFLD per symptoms

4 · Gynecologic management

  • Endometrial protection (3× CA risk): withdrawal bleed ≥ every 90 days — OCP, progestin IUD, or cyclic progesterone. No routine US screening.
  • Hirsutism/cycles: combined OCP (less-androgenic progestin: norgestimate, desogestrel, drospirenone) ± spironolactone 50→100 mg BID (teratogenic — contraception required).
  • Infertility (most common anovulatory cause): 5–10% weight loss; letrozole/clomiphene for ovulation; refer OB-Gyn/REI early.
  • Pregnancy: ↑ early loss, GDM (BMI-independent), pre-eclampsia, preterm — monitor closely.

5 · 2023 guideline update (framework unchanged)

  • AMH may replace ultrasound for PCOM in ADULTS (not adolescents).
  • Irregular cycles + hyperandrogenism (~70%) → no US/AMH needed.
  • Adolescents: require BOTH hyperandrogenism + ovulatory dysfunction; no US/AMH.
  • Anti-obesity pharmacotherapy incl. GLP-1 RA may be considered for weight (limited PCOS evidence).
