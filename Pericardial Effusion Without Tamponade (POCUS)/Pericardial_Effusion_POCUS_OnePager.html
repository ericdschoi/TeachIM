Pericardial Effusion & Tamponade — POCUS Quick Reference

PLAX-based identification · differential · tamponade assessment. TeachIM 5-minute POCUS case · CC BY-NC 4.0 · clinician-reviewed.

One line: On the parasternal long axis (PLAX), a pericardial effusion lies anterior to the descending thoracic aorta; a pleural effusion lies posterior to it. Most effusions do not tamponade — judge by clinical signs, not size.

Identify on PLAX

  • Pericardial = anterior to descending aorta; tracks between heart and aorta.
  • Pleural = posterior to descending aorta.
  • Subxiphoid view = most sensitive window for effusion.
  • Always confirm any cardiac POCUS finding in a 2nd view.
  • Note chamber sizes & LV function; circumferential vs loculated.

Differential by Setting

Infectious (more common)
Viral — most common in developed world (Coxsackie, echovirus, HHV6, parvo B19, COVID-19)
TB — >70% in developing world
Non-infectious (less common)
Autoimmune · malignancy · uremia · post-trauma/post-MI · radiation · hypothyroidism

Significance = Size × Chronicity × Clinical

  • Size: trace may be normal; interpret in context.
  • Chronicity: compare to prior echo.
  • Tamponade is dynamic — driven by rate of accumulation, not absolute volume (2025 ESC). Small + rapid can tamponade; large + chronic may not.

Tamponade — 3 Key Findings

SignFreq %
Elevated JVP100
Tachycardia81–100
Pulsus paradoxus >10 mmHg98
Beck's triad (JVP↑ + ↓BP + muffled tones)0–19
  • Pulsus paradoxus = inspiratory SBP drop >10 mmHg.
  • Higher cutoff → ↑specificity, ↓sensitivity.
  • LR+ >10 mmHg: 3.3 · >12 mmHg: 5.9.
  • Beck's triad rarely present → do not rely on it.

2025 ESC Update

  • 2015 ESC pericardial guideline → 2025 ESC myocarditis & pericarditis guideline.
  • Pericardiocentesis first-line for significant effusion/tamponade; surgical window for recurrent/loculated/purulent.
  • CMR (Class I) if dx unclear/myocarditis suspected; cardiac CT (Class I) for loculated/complex effusion, calcification, masses.
  • New umbrella concept: IMPS (Inflammatory Myopericardial Syndrome).

Bedside Bottom Line

  • Confirm location (ant vs post to aorta) in a 2nd view.
  • Effusion present? → ask: JVP↑? tachy? pulsus >10?
  • No tamponade signs → treat the cause (often viral/supportive).
  • Tamponade signs → urgent echo + pericardiocentesis.
