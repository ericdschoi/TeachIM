SVT — Clinical One-Pager

Sort 7 SVTs by regularity + P waves · 2015 ACC/AHA/HRS & 2019 ESC · IM resident reference

1 · FRAMEWORK

Every tachycardia = increased automaticity (node/ectopic focus) or re-entry circuit (re-entry is faster). Then sort with two questions: regular vs irregular and the P waves.
Stable, unsure? Slow AV conduction (vagal or adenosine) to expose hidden atrial activity — saw-tooth, retro-P, or organized atrial tach.

3 · THREE MANAGEMENT BUCKETS

  • Address the driver: sinus tach, atrial tach, MAT
  • Interrupt re-entry (vagal / adenosine): AVNRT, AVRT
  • Rate control (AV-nodal blockers): AF, atrial flutter
  • Unstable at any point → synchronized cardioversion

4 · ADENOSINE & VAGAL

  • Vagal first: modified (REVERT) Valsalva — strain into 10-mL syringe, then supine + passive leg raise (~43% vs ~17%).
  • Adenosine 6 → 12 → 12 mg rapid push + flush: terminates AVNRT/AVRT, unmasks flutter/atrial tach, no effect on VT — diagnostic + therapeutic.

SAFETY / AVOID

Pre-excited AF (WPW): irregular + wide + very fast. AVOID AV-nodal blockers (adenosine, CCB, β-blocker, digoxin) → can accelerate accessory-pathway conduction → VF. Give procainamide or synchronized cardioversion.
MAT: avoid non-selective β-blockers with active bronchospasm; correct K/Mg.
VT clue: wide-complex with extreme right-axis (positive aVR) favors VT — when in doubt, treat as VT.
