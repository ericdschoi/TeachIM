Outpatient non-acute chest pain — what is the clinical question?	Is this symptom due to obstructive (>50%) CAD? (The ED question is instead: is this ACS, and will aggressive inpatient care reduce short-term MACE?)
What does the CAD Consortium Clinical Score estimate, and from what inputs?	Pretest probability of obstructive CAD, from age, sex, chest-pain character, and risk factors (DM, HTN, dyslipidemia, smoking). Outperforms Diamond–Forrester.
The three pretest-probability bands and the action for each?	Low <5% → seek alternative cause (no stress test). Intermediate 5–70% → stress test. High >70% → ECG + imaging to exclude high-grade disease, start empiric medical therapy.
For a high-pretest patient, what is the purpose of testing?	Not to diagnose CAD (already likely) but to exclude high-grade disease — left main, proximal LAD, or 3-vessel disease / significant cardiomyopathy — that carries sudden-death risk.
First branch point in choosing a stress test?	Can the patient exercise? Yes → exercise test (adds functional info, reproduces symptom). No → pharmacologic stress.
When can you use exercise treadmill ECG (ETT) vs. needing imaging?	ETT requires the ability to exercise AND an interpretable baseline ECG. Uninterpretable ECG (LBBB, paced, pre-excitation, LVH w/ strain, >1 mm ST-dep) → use imaging stress.
Why is ETT the preferred first functional test when feasible?	Cheap, gives functional capacity (METs) and prognostic data, and reproduces the symptom under observation.
Stress echo vs. nuclear MPI — how to choose?	Institution-dependent; both give EF and detect ischemia. Echo adds valve assessment; nuclear may be preferred with poor echo windows/body habitus.
"Markedly positive" exercise/ECG findings?	Significant ST depression at low workload, ST elevation, or exertional hypotension.
"Markedly positive" imaging findings?	Resting EF <35%, EF reduction or LV dilation with stress, or ischemia in >2 vascular territories.
After a positive (not markedly positive) stress test, next step?	Start medical management of stable CAD; defer coronary anatomy (CCTA or LHC) unless markedly positive or medical therapy fails.
Empiric medical therapy buckets for stable CAD?	Risk-factor modification (diet/exercise, smoking cessation, A1c <7, BP <130/80); cardioprotective (ASA 81, high-intensity statin, ACE-I/ARB if DM/CKD/EF<40%); anti-anginal (SL nitro PRN, beta-blocker, long-acting nitrate or CCB, consider ranolazine).
2021 Chest Pain Guideline — how should chest pain be described?	Cardiac / possibly cardiac / noncardiac — "typical/atypical" is discouraged.
2021 guideline — role of coronary artery calcium (CAC) in low-risk stable patients?	Useful first-line gatekeeper; CAC = 0 supports a low risk of obstructive CAD and deferral of further testing.
2021 guideline — status of CCTA for intermediate-high risk stable chest pain?	Class 1 first-line anatomic test (alongside stress imaging). CCTA favored if <65 y or less obstructive CAD suspected; stress favored if >65 y or more obstructive CAD suspected.
ISCHEMIA trial bottom line for stable CAD with moderate–severe ischemia?	An initial invasive strategy did not reduce death/MI vs. optimal medical therapy; revascularization improves angina-related quality of life. Treat medically first; revascularize for refractory symptoms or high-risk anatomy.
