SYNCOPE — Clinical One-Pager

Transient LOC from global cerebral hypoperfusion, w/ rapid spontaneous recovery · ACC/AHA 2017 & ESC 2018 · IM resident reference

1 · DEFINE & EXCLUDE MIMICS

Syncope = abrupt transient LOC, global cerebral hypoperfusion, rapid full recovery (6–8 s of ↓flow suffices). Subset of TLOC (short, abnl motor control, unresponsive, amnesia).
MimicTell-tale
SeizureProlonged convulsions, post-ictal confusion, lat. tongue bite, incontinence (convulsive syncope = brief jerks, no post-ictal state)
TIA/CVAPosterior circ. — LOC only with focal signs (vertigo, dysarthria); never isolated
HypoglycemiaImpaired (not lost) consciousness; longer; no recovery w/o Rx
OtherIntoxication, psychogenic pseudosyncope, concussion

2 · THREE CLASSIFICATIONS

Cascade: CPP = MAP − ICP; MAP = CO × SVR; CO = HR × SV (SV ← preload, afterload, contractility). Every cause is a lesion in this loop.
TypeDefect / causes
Orthostatic (insufficient response)↓SBP ≥20 or ↓DBP ≥10 on standing. Sympathetic (neurogenic: Parkinson/MSA, DM, amyloid) · SVR (drugs = #1 cause) · HR (BB/CCB) · preload (volume loss, diuretics, hemorrhage)
Reflex (inappropriate response)Vasovagal (orthostatic/emotional) · situational (micturition, defecation, cough, post-exercise) · carotid sinus hypersens. Subtypes: cardioinhibitory (↓HR), vasodepressive (↓SVR), mixed
Cardiac (the one that kills)↑Afterload (AS, HOCM) · ↓contractility (MI, VT) · ↓HR (AVB, SND, PM malfx) · ↓preload (PE, tamponade, pulm HTN, dissection)
PE myth: 17% in selected hospitalized cohort (Prandoni 2016), but <1% of all-comers & <3% hospitalized (2018, >1.5M pts). Uncommon — don't CT every fainter.

3 · EVALUATION — HISTORY DOES THE WORK

~60% of TLOC sorted by H&P alone. Who / Where-how / Prodrome:
OHReflexCardiac
WhoOlder, comorbid, autonomicAny age, healthy, recurrent <40Structural HD/CAD, FHx SCD
WhereOn/after standing, post-prandialStanding, hot/crowded, triggerSupine/rest (arrhythmia), exertion (structural)
ProdromeLightheaded w/ posturePallor, diaphoresis, nauseaNone, or sudden palpitations
Everyone gets 4: History · exam (cardiac) · orthostatic vitals (within 3 min) · 12-lead ECG.

3b · TARGETED TESTING

  • OH: BMP/CBC (volume, occult bleed); autonomic eval if neurogenic (Valsalva w/o HR/BP rise)
  • Reflex: hx dx; tilt-table if unclear (cardioinhibitory predicts asystolic syncope); carotid massage in >40 w/ suggestive hx (contra: bruit, stenosis >70%, TIA/CVA <3 mo)
  • Cardiac: tele, TTE (if structural susp.), ±trop/BNP, stress if exertional + CAD risk, CTPA only if PE susp.

AVOID in simple syncope (nl neuro, no trauma, no szr features): head CT/MRI, EEG, carotid US — yield ~0.2–1.5%; routine TTE/CTPA/broad labs.

5 · MANAGEMENT

  • OH: fluids, stop/adjust culprit meds, slow position change, salt, compression, counter-pressure maneuvers; midodrine/fludrocortisone if autonomic
  • Reflex: reassurance, trigger avoidance, counter-pressure if prodrome; ±midodrine/fludro/salt; pacemaker select (>40 + documented cardioinhibitory + frequent/unpredictable)
  • Cardiac: consult cardiology/EP — etiology-specific (EF ≤35% → ICD discussion)
2026: 2021 ESC pacing guideline + BIOSync RCT (77% RRR @2y) strengthened DDD-CLS pacing for >40 w/ severe recurrent cardioinhibitory reflex syncope + documented asystole; cardioneuroablation emerging (device-free).
