Transplant Medicine — Immunosuppression & Post-Transplant Complications
Balancing rejection vs. infection/cancer/toxicity · induction vs. maintenance · complications by time from transplant. IM facilitator reference.

The Core Balance

More immunosuppression → less rejection, but more infection, malignancy, drug toxicity. Therapy is titrated to each organ's rejection risk and time from transplant.

Allogenicity (rejection risk): Lung > heart/kidney > liver. Lung ~50% reject by 5 yr (often on all 3 classes for life); liver least (may be on CNI alone).

Maintenance classes: CNI (tacrolimus, cyclosporine) · antimetabolite (mycophenolate, azathioprine) · steroid (prednisone) · mTOR inhibitor (sirolimus, everolimus).

Rejection

  • Acute cellular (T-cell): biopsy = lymphocytic infiltrate → pulse methylpred 1 g ×3 d ± anti-lymphocyte Ab.
  • Acute humoral (Ab-mediated): biopsy C4d / donor-specific Abs → ↑ immunosuppression, IVIg, plasmapheresis, rituximab, bortezomib, eculizumab.
  • Chronic: lung = bronchiolitis obliterans (obstructive, ↓FEV1; azithromycin 3×/wk); kidney = chronic allograft dysfunction (↑Cr, proteinuria).
  • First acute rejection >6 mo unlikely if adherent (~25% have an acute episode overall).

Drug Toxicities & Malignancy

  • CNI: nephro/neurotoxicity, ↑K, ↑uric acid, metabolic syndrome.
  • Antimetabolite: marrow suppression, GI (colitis). Steroid: metabolic syndrome.
  • Cancer ~2× general population — virally driven (PTLD/EBV early; skin/anogenital HPV, liver late).

Newer Context (cite before teaching)

  • Belatacept (Nulojix, 2011): CD28 costimulation blocker, CNI-sparing; better long-term GFR. Contraindicated if EBV-seronegative (PTLD/PML).
  • Letermovir (2023): CMV prophylaxis in high-risk D+/R– kidney Tx; less myelosuppression than valganciclovir. Maribavir (2021): refractory/resistant CMV.

High-Yield Pearls

  • A NEW fever in a transplant patient = think infection AND rejection; both can present with malaise and graft dysfunction.
  • Match the bug to the clock: nosocomial <1 mo, opportunistic/reactivation 1–6 mo, community-acquired >6 mo.
  • CNI levels are critical: too low → rejection; too high → nephrotoxicity. Watch drug–drug interactions (azoles, macrolides, diltiazem ↑ levels).
  • Standard prophylaxis 1–6 mo: TMP-SMX (PJP), valganciclovir or letermovir (CMV per donor/recipient serostatus).
