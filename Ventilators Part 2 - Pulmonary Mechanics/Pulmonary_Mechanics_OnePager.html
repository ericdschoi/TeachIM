Pulmonary Mechanics in the Ventilated Patient

High peak-pressure alarm: establish urgency → get a plateau (inspiratory hold) → two subtractions tell you resistance vs compliance. Volume-targeted modes. Adapted from TeachIM.org (McLeod/Scudder; Steinbach; Zhang, Apr 2023), CC BY-NC 4.0.

Key Outputs: set vs monitor

ModeMonitor (outputs)
Volume-targetedPeak & plateau (driving) pressures
Pressure-targetedVT & minute ventilation VE

VE = VT × RR. Plateau measured on an inspiratory hold (no flow).

The two subtractions

MechanicFormulaNormal
ResistancePpeak − Pplateau<10
Compliance (driving P)Pplateau − PEEP<10*

*for a 4–6 mL/kg breath. Plateau goal <30. ARDS driving-pressure goal ≤14–15 (Amato 2015).

Peak = the "everything" pressure (R + C + PEEP + insp flow). Plateau has no flow → resistance drops out → reflects lung/chest-wall only.

Step 1 — Establish urgency

Emergency = high peak PLUS any of:

  • SpO2 falling
  • VT low / VE down >50%
  • Rising PCO2
  • Dropping BP

→ Call for help + FiO2 100%, work the steps in parallel. Don't delay for an ABG.

Step 2 — Assess resistance

Ppeak − Pplateau > 10 = resistance problem.

CauseAction
Kinked tubingInspect / uncompress
Biting ETTBite block + sedation/analgesia
Mucus plug / obstructionDeep suction (± bronch)
BronchospasmBronchodilators
Refractory kink/blockExchange ETT (airway-trained)

Step 3 — Assess compliance

Pplateau > 30 or driving > 10 = compliance problem.

BucketCauses
Alveolar fillingEdema, ARDS, pneumonia, DAH
Alveolar collapseAtelectasis, lobar collapse
ExtrapulmonaryPTX, pleural effusion, ascites, abdominal distention/ACS, stiff chest wall

Action: history, targeted exam, CXR/POCUS, ABG; bladder pressure if abdominal compartment syndrome suspected.

Tempo & pearls

  • PTX: spike over min–hrs · ARDS/fibrosis: climb over hrs–days · COPD: air-trapping (auto-PEEP)
  • R and C problems can co-exist
  • Auto-PEEP / breath-stacking + hypotension → disconnect circuit to allow full exhalation, then slow the rate & treat bronchospasm
  • Worked Case 1: peak 40, plateau 38, PEEP 12 → R = 40−38 = 2 (normal); driving = 38−12 = 26 (high) → low compliance (worsening ARDS)

2023–25 framing

  • Driving pressure is the vent variable most tied to ARDS survival — keep ≤14–15 even when VT/plateau already protective (Amato NEJM 2015)
  • Lung-protective: VT 4–6 mL/kg IBW, plateau <30, PEEP >5 (ARDSNet 2000; ATS/ESICM/SCCM 2017; ESICM 2023)
  • Global ARDS def 2024: SpO2/FiO2 ≤315, lung US, HFNO recognized
