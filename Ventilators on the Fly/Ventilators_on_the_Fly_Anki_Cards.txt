The 4 questions for reading any ventilator?	(1) What mode? (2) What level of O2 support? (3) Is ventilation appropriate? (4) How are the respiratory mechanics?
Dials vs measured values on the screen?	Right-side dials = clinician-set; left-side numbers = ventilator-measured. They differ when the patient breathes over the set rate.
Square wave in PRESSURE means…?	A pressure-targeted mode: PC, PRVC, or PS.
Square wave in FLOW means…?	Classic volume control (modern vents can change the flow shape, so its absence does not exclude VC).
Control vs support mode — the tell?	Control mode = a set/mandatory RR. Support mode (PS) = no mandatory RR; patient triggers all breaths.
Pressure Control — target and monitor?	Target Pcontrol/deltaP; monitor tidal volume and minute ventilation.
Volume Control — target and monitor?	Target tidal volume; monitor peak and plateau pressure. Used for ARDS lung-protective VT.
PRVC — what is it?	Hybrid: set a target VT; the vent algorithm sets the pressure to achieve it. Square pressure wave; monitor peak/plateau + achieved VT.
Pressure Support — use and risk?	Spontaneous mode for SBTs/minimal support; no mandatory RR, so apnea triggers a backup control mode.
Which 2 settings set oxygenation in every mode?	FiO2 and PEEP (read against the ARDSNet PEEP/FiO2 ladder).
Minute ventilation equation and normal range?	VE = VT x RR. Normal 4-6 L/min (often higher in disease).
Target pH range when judging ventilation?	Generally keep pH 7.2-7.5.
Patient-driven tachypnea — causes to look for?	CNS injury/suppression, pain, salicylates, metabolic acidosis.
Volume mode, deltaP > 10 — next step?	Perform an inspiratory hold to obtain the plateau pressure.
Peak minus Plateau > 3-5 indicates…?	A RESISTANCE problem: mucus plug, kinked tube, bronchospasm.
Elevated Plateau minus PEEP (>10) indicates…?	A COMPLIANCE problem: pneumothorax, alveolar filling/edema, fibrosis, mainstem intubation, distended abdomen.
