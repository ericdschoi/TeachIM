First branch point in any anemia work-up?	Reticulocyte index: <2 = underproduction (marrow); >=3 = overproduction (blood loss, hemolysis, sequestration).
What drops Hgb without deranging the retic index?	Hemodilution -- aggressive IVF (inpatient) or hypervolemia/HF (outpatient).
Caveat to the retic index?	May be falsely low/normal if an underproduction process coexists with bleeding/hemolysis.
Macrocytic underproduction causes?	Megaloblastic (B12/folate, drugs: HU/allopurinol/HIV meds), liver/ETOH, severe hypothyroidism, primary marrow (MDS, esp. MCV >120).
Microcytic underproduction causes?	Iron deficiency, thalassemia, sideroblastic (lead, Zn, INH/linezolid), anemia of chronic inflammation.
When do you shift toward primary marrow pathology?	When other cell lines are also affected (MDS, aplastic anemia, leukemia, myeloma).
Iron studies in iron deficiency?	Low Fe, high TIBC, low %sat (<20% suggestive, <10% very), low ferritin (<30 ng/mL diagnostic).
Iron studies in anemia of chronic disease?	Low Fe, low-normal TIBC, low-normal %sat, normal-high ferritin (acute-phase reactant).
Does a low ferritin confirm iron deficiency in inflammation?	Yes -- inflammation only raises ferritin; ferritin <30 is diagnostic even with active inflammation.
ACD + concurrent iron deficiency -- how common and how to tell?	20-85% of ACD. Clue: very low TSAT; confirm with sTfR, hepcidin, or response to an iron trial.
Labs that confirm hemolysis?	High LDH, low haptoglobin, high indirect bilirubin, + peripheral smear.
What does the DAT (direct Coombs) distinguish?	Immune (DAT-positive, antibodies on RBCs -> AIHA) vs non-immune (DAT-negative) hemolysis.
Warm vs cold AIHA?	Warm = IgG (cancer/lymphoma, SLE, drugs); Cold = IgM (lymphoma, mycoplasma, mono; also PNH, syphilis). Smear: spherocytes.
Smear clue for MAHA, and what it means with low platelets?	Schistocytes; with thrombocytopenia -> thrombotic microangiopathy (TTP/HUS) -- an emergency.
Primary vs secondary TMA?	Primary: TTP/HUS, drug-induced. Secondary/TMA-like: DIC (low fibrinogen), HELLP. Other: malignant HTN, prosthetic valve/LVAD/TIPS, scleroderma renal crisis.
Suspected TTP -- what to do?	PLASMIC score, send ADAMTS13 BEFORE plasma exchange, start PEX if TTP likely; treat confirmed TTP with PEX + steroids + caplacizumab.
Intrinsic non-immune (non-MAHA) hemolysis categories?	Membrane (HS, elliptocytosis, stomatocytosis, PNH), Hb (sickle, thalassemia), metabolism (G6PD, pyruvate kinase deficiency).
New iron deficiency in an older adult -- next step?	GI evaluation for occult blood loss (colon cancer until proven otherwise).
