Tool to predict complicated withdrawal on admission?	PAWSS (Prediction of Alcohol Withdrawal Severity Scale); &ge;4 flags risk.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Withdrawal timeline?	Minor sx 6–24 h; seizures 12–48 h; hallucinosis 12–48 h; delirium tremens 48–96 h.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Hallucinosis vs DTs?	Hallucinosis = hallucinations with CLEAR sensorium + near-normal vitals; DTs = altered sensorium + autonomic instability.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
First-line pharmacotherapy?	Benzodiazepines — reduce seizures and DTs (ASAM).	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Benefit of symptom-triggered (CIWA-Ar) dosing?	Less total benzodiazepine and shorter treatment vs fixed schedules in assessable patients.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
When is CIWA-Ar invalid?	In patients who can't communicate or whose confusion has another cause — don't titrate off it.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Benzo choice in significant liver disease?	Lorazepam (no active metabolites); long-acting agents (diazepam/chlordiazepoxide) self-taper otherwise.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Treatment for severe / benzo-resistant withdrawal?	Phenobarbital — long half-life, self-tapering, acts on GABA and glutamate.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Do clonidine/dexmedetomidine/antipsychotics prevent seizures?	No — they treat autonomic/agitation symptoms only; keep a GABAergic agent on board.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Thiamine vs glucose order?	Thiamine before/with glucose — dextrose first can precipitate Wernicke encephalopathy.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Key electrolytes to replete?	Magnesium, potassium, phosphate; magnesium supports the seizure threshold.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Front-loading strategy — when?	High-risk/severe presentations — large early doses of a long-acting benzodiazepine.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
ICU transfer triggers?	Delirium tremens, airway risk, escalating sedation requirements.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Discharge step for the underlying disorder?	Offer naltrexone/acamprosate and link to addiction treatment.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
Mortality of untreated DTs?	Meaningful — DTs are a medical emergency; undertreatment is dangerous.	General::alcohol-withdrawal
DKA vs HHS — glucose and ketones?	DKA: ketoacidosis, glucose ≥200 (can be lower). HHS: glucose >600, minimal ketosis, effective osm >320, pH >7.3.	General::dka-hhs
2024 ADA consensus DKA bicarbonate cutoff?	Lowered to 15 mmol/L (to allow for insulinopenic acidosis).	General::dka-hhs
What is euglycemic DKA?	Gap acidosis + ketones with near-normal glucose — SGLT2 inhibitors, pregnancy, starvation.	General::dka-hhs
Preferred initial resuscitation fluid (2024)?	Balanced crystalloid (LR/Plasma-Lyte) over saline.	General::dka-hhs
The potassium gate?	K <3.3: hold insulin, replace K first. K 3.3–5.2: insulin + add K. K >5.2: insulin, recheck, hold K.	General::dka-hhs
Why is total-body K depleted despite normal serum K?	Acidosis shifts K out of cells; insulin then drives it back in — anticipate the fall.	General::dka-hhs
When add dextrose in DKA?	When glucose ~200–250, to keep insulin running and clear ketones.	General::dka-hhs
What do you treat to in DKA — glucose or the gap?	The anion gap / ketones, not glucose alone.	General::dka-hhs
DKA resolution criteria?	Glucose <200 AND two of: bicarb ≥15–18, venous pH >7.3, anion gap normalized.	General::dka-hhs
When is bicarbonate indicated?	Only if pH <7.0 — not routine.	General::dka-hhs
When is phosphate replaced?	Only if <1.0 mmol/L with muscle weakness or cardiac/respiratory compromise.	General::dka-hhs
Insulin route by severity?	IV infusion for moderate/severe DKA and HHS; SC rapid-acting protocols for mild uncomplicated DKA.	General::dka-hhs
How to transition off the insulin drip safely?	Give SC long-acting insulin 1–2 h BEFORE stopping the drip to prevent rebound ketoacidosis.	General::dka-hhs
Common DKA/HHS precipitants (the I's)?	Infection, Infarction (MI), Insulin omission, Ischemia, Iatrogenic (steroids/SGLT2i); also new-onset DM, pregnancy.	General::dka-hhs
Why not stop the drip when only glucose is normal?	The gap may still be open — stopping insulin restarts ketoacidosis.	General::dka-hhs
Is S. aureus in a blood culture ever a contaminant?	No — always significant; treat as true bacteremia.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
The five core interventions for SAB?	ID consult, echocardiography, repeat blood cultures (clearance), source control, appropriate drug & duration.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Empiric therapy for suspected SAB?	Vancomycin (covers MRSA) while awaiting susceptibilities.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Preferred therapy for MSSA bacteremia?	Cefazolin or an antistaphylococcal penicillin (nafcillin/oxacillin) — both beat vancomycin.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Why not keep MSSA on vancomycin?	Vancomycin is inferior for MSSA — higher failure and mortality; de-escalate.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Therapy for MRSA bacteremia?	Vancomycin (AUC-guided) or daptomycin.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Criteria for UNCOMPLICATED SAB (all required)?	Negative follow-up cultures, defervescence within 72 h, no endocarditis/prosthetic material, no metastatic foci.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Duration for uncomplicated SAB?	≥14 days IV from the first negative blood culture.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Duration for complicated SAB?	4–6 weeks or longer (endocarditis, osteomyelitis, hardware).	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
When does the treatment clock start?	From the first negative (clearance) blood culture, not the first positive.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Common metastatic foci of S. aureus?	Endocarditis, vertebral osteomyelitis/discitis, epidural abscess, septic arthritis, psoas/splenic abscess, septic emboli.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
TTE vs TEE in SAB?	TEE is more sensitive for endocarditis; pursue with prosthetic valve/device, persistent bacteremia, community-onset, or unclear source.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Why repeat blood cultures in SAB?	To document clearance — persistent positives indicate complicated infection and reset duration.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
Does ID consultation affect outcomes in SAB?	Yes — independently improves survival; recommended for every case.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
New back pain + persistent bacteremia in SAB suggests?	Vertebral osteomyelitis/discitis ± epidural abscess — image with MRI; complicated SAB.	General::s-aureus-bacteremia
