Tool to predict complicated withdrawal on admission?	PAWSS (Prediction of Alcohol Withdrawal Severity Scale); &ge;4 flags risk.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Withdrawal timeline?	Minor sx 6–24 h; seizures 12–48 h; hallucinosis 12–48 h; delirium tremens 48–96 h.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Hallucinosis vs DTs?	Hallucinosis = hallucinations with CLEAR sensorium + near-normal vitals; DTs = altered sensorium + autonomic instability.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
First-line pharmacotherapy?	Benzodiazepines — reduce seizures and DTs (ASAM).	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Benefit of symptom-triggered (CIWA-Ar) dosing?	Less total benzodiazepine and shorter treatment vs fixed schedules in assessable patients.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
When is CIWA-Ar invalid?	In patients who can't communicate or whose confusion has another cause — don't titrate off it.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Benzo choice in significant liver disease?	Lorazepam (no active metabolites); long-acting agents (diazepam/chlordiazepoxide) self-taper otherwise.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Treatment for severe / benzo-resistant withdrawal?	Phenobarbital — long half-life, self-tapering, acts on GABA and glutamate.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Do clonidine/dexmedetomidine/antipsychotics prevent seizures?	No — they treat autonomic/agitation symptoms only; keep a GABAergic agent on board.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Thiamine vs glucose order?	Thiamine before/with glucose — dextrose first can precipitate Wernicke encephalopathy.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Key electrolytes to replete?	Magnesium, potassium, phosphate; magnesium supports the seizure threshold.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Front-loading strategy — when?	High-risk/severe presentations — large early doses of a long-acting benzodiazepine.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
ICU transfer triggers?	Delirium tremens, airway risk, escalating sedation requirements.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Discharge step for the underlying disorder?	Offer naltrexone/acamprosate and link to addiction treatment.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Mortality of untreated DTs?	Meaningful — DTs are a medical emergency; undertreatment is dangerous.	Psychiatry::alcohol-withdrawal
Why screen for depression in primary care?	~50% of clinical depression is missed without screening; untreated depression worsens quality of life, mortality, and economic burden. USPSTF 2023: screen all adults (Grade B).	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
PHQ-2 / PHQ-4 vs PHQ-9 / GAD-7 — when to use which?	PHQ-2 and PHQ-4 are brief SCREENS for general populations. PHQ-9 (depression) and GAD-7 (anxiety) DIAGNOSE and MONITOR response — use them when pretest probability is high.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
PHQ-9 score interpretation?	5–9 mild, 10–14 moderate, 15–19 moderately severe, ≥20 severe; ≥10 = likely major depression. It measures severity/trends response but does not by itself diagnose.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
DSM-5 criteria for unipolar major depression?	≥5 of 9 symptoms for ≥2 weeks nearly every day, and MUST include depressed mood or loss of interest/pleasure; symptoms cause functional impairment and aren't due to a substance/medical condition.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
DSM-5 criteria for generalized anxiety disorder?	Excessive, hard-to-control anxiety more days than not for ≥6 months about multiple events, plus ≥3 symptoms (restlessness, fatigue, poor concentration, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance), with functional impairment.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
Which screening labs to consider for new depression/anxiety?	No routine panel is mandated. Consider CBC (anemia), TSH (thyroid), HCG (pregnancy — also informs drug safety), and BMP (renal dosing). Rule out medical/psychiatric mimics.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
Clues that raise suspicion for bipolar disorder before starting an antidepressant?	Age <25, family history of bipolar disorder, and multiple (≥3–5) recurrences of major depression. Antidepressants can unmask mania/hypomania.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
First-line treatment for both GAD and unipolar depression?	SRIs (especially SSRIs) — comparable response to psychotherapy, and combination is additive. Pick the agent by comorbidity, prior response, cost, preference, and side-effect profile.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
Why is short-term follow-up after starting an antidepressant critical?	The first 1–2 weeks carry risk of emergent suicidality or mania, intolerable (often transient) side effects, and early discontinuation. Early contact improves safety and adherence.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
Typical antidepressant treatment timeline / response check?	First follow-up at 1–2 weeks (confirm no worsening suicidality/mania or intolerable side effects); second at 2–4 weeks to assess response. Increase dose, switch, or maintain accordingly; consider discontinuation at 3–9 months.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
Which SSRI is preferred in a patient on many medications, and why?	Citalopram — relatively few CYP P450 drug–drug interactions (note dose-dependent QTc prolongation; cap 40 mg, 20 mg in elderly).	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
When is mirtazapine a useful choice?	When weight gain and sedation are desirable (e.g., an older patient with poor appetite and insomnia).	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
Which antidepressant to avoid if a patient is concerned about weight gain, and which SSRI has the longest half-life?	Avoid paroxetine (weight gain, anticholinergic, short half-life → withdrawal). Fluoxetine has the longest half-life (less withdrawal, needs no taper).	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
When might an SNRI be first-line, and what's a hidden risk of dramatic improvement after a switch?	SNRI (e.g., duloxetine, venlafaxine) when comorbid chronic pain. A sudden "better than ever"/high-energy response can signal unmasked bipolar disorder — reassess the diagnosis.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
How should antidepressants be stopped, and what does ANTLER (NEJM 2021) show?	Taper slowly (withdrawal can be prolonged). ANTLER: discontinuation after long-term use → ~56% relapse vs ~39% with maintenance — relapse is common either way.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
How should you ask about suicide, and does asking increase risk?	Use clear, direct language ("Are you having thoughts of killing yourself right now?"). Asking does NOT increase suicide risk or rates. Assess at each visit; know your site's safety plan and resources.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
USPSTF 2023 screening recommendations for depression and anxiety in adults?	Screen ALL adults for depression (Grade B) and adults <65 for anxiety disorders (Grade B), including pregnant/postpartum. Insufficient evidence for anxiety screening at ≥65 (I).	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
Newer oral antidepressant agents (2022–2023) and their niche?	Dextromethorphan–bupropion (Auvelity, FDA 2022) — rapid-acting oral NMDA antagonist for MDD. Zuranolone (Zurzuvae, FDA 2023) — first oral agent for postpartum depression (14-day course), not general MDD.	Psychiatry::depression-and-anxiety
